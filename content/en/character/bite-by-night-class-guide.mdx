## Survivor Class Tier List and Overview

In Bite By Night, survivors are tasked with completing objectives while avoiding the lethal reach of animatronic killers. Unlike many other horror games on the platform, your "class" provides specific active and passive abilities that can turn the tide of a chase. Choosing the right role is the difference between being a "Customer" waiting to be caught and a "Security Guard" controlling the flow of the match.

### S-Tier: Security Guard
The Security Guard is widely considered the most valuable member of any survivor team. Their kit is designed for both information gathering and active defense. Using the **Cams** ability, a Guard can check the position of the Killer without putting themselves in immediate danger. This is crucial for determining which generators are safe to work on.

When a confrontation is inevitable, the **Taser** provides a ranged stun. Even if the Killer has high tenacity, the Taser forces them into a slowed state, granting the Guard and their nearby teammates enough time to reach a vault point or hide.

### A-Tier: Fighter
For players who prefer a high-risk, high-reward playstyle, the Fighter is the premier choice. This class focuses on the **Parry** mechanic, which requires precise timing to deflect a Killer's strike. A successful parry inflicts a 2-second stun on the attacker, allowing for a clean getaway.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Mastering the Parry window is essential. If you miss the timing, you are left wide open for a massive damage hit. Use the **Swing** ability after a parry to apply debuffs to the Killer, making them less effective for the remainder of the chase.

### B-Tier: Medic
The Medic is the backbone of team longevity. In a game where health is a finite resource, the ability to grant 5 HP every 2 seconds via the **Heal** ability is game-changing. They also possess **Self-Heal**, though it is slightly less efficient than healing others. The primary drawback of the Medic is their lack of defensive tools; if a Killer targets the Medic first, they have very few ways to fight back.

### C-Tier: Customer
The Customer is the default "blank" class. While they have access to **Pizza** (heals 20% HP) and **Drink** (speed boost), these items come with significant downsides. The Pizza animation is slow, and the Drink provides a "sugar crash" debuff that slows the player down once the initial boost wears off. Most players use this class only to grind the initial Scrap needed to unlock better roles.

## Killer Class Rankings and Abilities

Playing as the Killer in Bite By Night requires a different mindset. You are the power role, but you are outnumbered. To succeed, you must utilize your unique animatronic powers to disrupt survivor coordination. As of early 2026, there are three primary killers available, each with a distinct complexity level.

| Killer | Title | Playstyle | Key Ability |

| **Mimic** | The Project | Versatile / Technical | **Mode Switch** |
| **Springtrap** | The Rotten | Basic / Chaser | **Remnant Cleaver** |

### Ennard (The Doppelganger) - S Rank
Ennard is currently the strongest killer for those who enjoy psychological warfare. Their **Skin Stealer** ability allows them to take on the appearance of a survivor they have recently eliminated. In a chaotic lobby, this allows Ennard to walk right up to generators and catch survivors off-guard.

Beyond deception, Ennard is a powerhouse. Their **Grab** ability prevents survivors from fleeing, while **Wire Eyes** allows them to spawn slow-moving robot slugs. These slugs latch onto survivors, preventing them from running until they are shaken off, making them easy prey for Ennard’s high-damage melee attacks.

### Mimic (The Project) - A Rank
The Mimic is perhaps the most complex character in this **bite by night class guide**. It features a **Mode Switch** mechanic that allows the player to toggle between three distinct states:
1.  **Strength Mode:** Increases melee damage significantly, often downing survivors in 2-3 hits.
2.  **Speed Mode:** Enhances movement speed for closing gaps in open areas.
3.  **Stealth Mode:** Silences footsteps, making it the perfect tool for ambushing the Security Guard.

While the Mimic is incredibly powerful, it requires constant micromanagement. A player who forgets to switch back to Speed mode during a chase will likely lose their target.

### Springtrap (The Rotten) - B Rank
Springtrap is the "starter" killer, but don't let the B-rank fool you—he is still dangerous in the right hands. His kit is straightforward: he chases, he hits, and he traps. His **Remnant Cleaver** is a projectile axe throw that can injure survivors from a distance, though it requires lead-time to hit a sprinting target.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Springtrap's **Beartraps** are easily spotted in open hallways. To maximize their effectiveness, place them directly behind doors or at the base of ladders where survivors are forced to travel.

## Scrap Farming and Progression

To unlock the higher-tier classes mentioned in this **bite by night class guide**, you will need to accumulate Scrap. Scrap is the primary currency in Bite By Night, earned by participating in matches, completing objectives, and damaging (or escaping) opponents.

*   **Survivor Earnings:** You can earn up to 500 Scrap and 3000 EXP per round as a survivor. Focus on repairing generators and successfully stunning the killer with a Taser or Parry to maximize your gains.
*   **Killer Earnings:** Killers earn Scrap based on total damage dealt and the number of survivors eliminated.
*   **VIP Pass:** If you are looking to speed up your progression, the VIP pass offers a multiplier on all Scrap earned, which is highly recommended for players looking to unlock Ennard or the Mimic quickly.

## Advanced Strategies for 2026

1.  **The Buddy System:** If you are playing as a Medic or a Fighter, never wander alone. A Fighter can protect a Medic while they heal, creating a nearly unkillable duo against lower-tier killers like Springtrap.
2.  **Information Control:** If you are the Security Guard, communicate the Killer's location constantly. Knowing which "mode" the Mimic is in can help your team decide whether to run or hide.
3.  **Kill the Medic First:** As a Killer, your priority target should always be the Medic. If you allow a Medic to stay alive, all the damage you deal to other survivors will be undone within seconds.
4.  **Manage Your Stamina:** Both Killers and Survivors rely on stamina for sprinting. If you exhaust your stamina bar during a chase, you are essentially a sitting duck. Learn to "feather" your sprint key to maintain a baseline of energy.

## FAQ

**Q: Which class should I unlock first in Bite By Night?**

A: For survivors, the Security Guard is the best first unlock due to its high utility and the ability to stun killers from a safe distance. For killers, save your Scrap for Ennard if you enjoy a deceptive playstyle, or the Mimic if you prefer versatility.

**Q: How do I counter Ennard's Skin Stealer ability?**

A: Pay close attention to player behavior. If you see a "teammate" walking toward a generator without using their flashlight or ignoring the objective, it is likely Ennard. Always keep a mental count of who has been eliminated.

**Q: What is the maximum amount of Scrap I can earn in a single match?**

A: As a survivor, the current cap is 500 Scrap per round. This is intended to keep the progression balanced and encourage players to play multiple matches to unlock the S-tier roles featured in this **bite by night class guide**.

**Q: Can the Fighter parry the Killer's special abilities?**

A: Generally, the Fighter's Parry only works on basic melee attacks. Projectiles like Springtrap's Remnant Cleaver or Ennard's Wire Eyes cannot be parried and must be dodged or shaken off manually.
