### 1. The Rotten (Springtrap)
The Rotten is often the first monster players encounter. Modeled after the iconic Springtrap, this killer excels at area denial. By placing barbwire traps in high-traffic areas—such as the security office or near generators—The Rotten can stun survivors and close the gap quickly.

*   **Axe Throw (Q):** A ranged attack that can damage and slow survivors from a distance.
*   **Barbwire Traps (E):** Deployable traps that highlight and stun players who step on them.
*   **Scream:** A powerful vocal ability that can reveal the location of nearby hiders.

> **💡 Tip:** When facing The Rotten, always keep your flashlight pointed at the ground in doorways. Traps are easier to spot with the "Hitbox" setting enabled in the game options.

### 2. The Project (The Mimic)
The Mimic is a high-mobility killer that relies on surprise attacks. Its ability to "drill" underground allows it to bypass barricades and pop up directly underneath unsuspecting survivors. While its damage in "Speed Mode" is lower per hit, its ability to stay on top of a target makes it a relentless pursuer.

*   **Drill Movement:** Move beneath the floor to ignore obstacles and detect survivor footsteps.
*   **Speed Mode:** Increases movement speed significantly at the cost of some attack power.
*   **Heart-Pull Execution:** A brutal close-range grab that can instantly eliminate low-health survivors.

### 3. The Doppelganger (Ennard)
Arguably the most complex killer in the **bite by night all monsters list**, The Doppelganger (Ennard) uses psychological warfare. It can take the form of any survivor in the lobby, including their username and avatar skin. This allows it to walk right up to a group of players working on a generator without raising suspicion.

*   **Skin Stealer:** After downing a survivor, Ennard can "eat" their costume to perfectly mimic their appearance.
*   **Mini-Dudes:** Small mechanical spiders that Ennard can throw. If they land on a survivor's face, they cause continuous damage and obscure vision.
*   **Flashlight Mimicry:** Unlike other killers, Ennard can use a survivor's flashlight while disguised to further the illusion.

### Survival Strategies
1.  **Objective Speed:** When repairing generators, you can hold the interaction button rather than tapping it to complete tasks significantly faster. This is a common trick used by veteran players to finish rounds before the Killer can build "Agony."
2.  **Agony System:** The Killer becomes stronger as the match progresses. If you spend too much time hiding and not completing tasks, the Killer's "Agony" meter will fill, granting them wall-hacks and increased damage.
3.  **Barricading:** Use chairs and boxes to block doors. While monsters can kick these down, it provides a 3-5 second window for survivors to escape through vents or windows.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not hide in the same spot for more than 60 seconds. The game features an "anti-camping" mechanic that will highlight your position to the monster if you remain stationary for too long.

## Unlocking New Monsters
Monsters are unlocked using **Scrap**, the primary in-game currency earned by completing matches, damaging the killer, or escaping. As of 2026, the costs for the **bite by night all monsters list** are as follows:

*   **The Rotten:** Default / Free
*   **The Project:** 4,500 Scrap
*   **The Doppelganger:** 6,000 Scrap

You can also purchase cosmetic skins, such as the **Spartan Skin** for The Rotten, which changes the execution animations and weapon models. Earning scrap is relatively fast; an average win as a survivor can net between 400 and 500 scrap, meaning you can unlock the top-tier killers in about 12-15 successful rounds.

## Map Knowledge: Pizzeria vs. Forest
The environment plays a massive role in which monster is most effective. The **Pizzeria** map is tight and claustrophobic, favoring killers like The Rotten who can trap narrow hallways. Conversely, the **Forest** map is open and dark, giving The Mimic plenty of space to use its speed and underground traversal.

For survivors, the Pizzeria offers "Safe Rooms" like the Security Office, which has cameras that can be used to track the monster's movement. In the Forest, survivors must rely on finding batteries to power the exit gates, which often requires venturing into the dangerous center of the map.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best killer in the Bite by Night all monsters list?**

A: Currently, **The Doppelganger (Ennard)** is considered the strongest due to its ability to mimic survivors and its "Mini-Dude" traps which track players automatically. However, it requires the most skill to play effectively.

**Q: How do I escape a grab from The Mimic?**

A: If you are grabbed, you must succeed in a "Struggle" mini-game by mashing the displayed keys. Having a **Fighter** teammate nearby can also help, as they can hit the monster to force a release.

**Q: Does the Pizza item heal you?**

A: In the current 2026 build, the Pizza item is a temporary buff that restores a small amount of stamina but does not restore health. To regain health, you must find a Medic or use a Medkit found in wall-mounted stations.

**Q: What is "Agony" in Bite by Night?**

A: Agony is a mechanic where the monster gains power the longer the game lasts or the more survivors they hit. At high Agony levels, killers gain permanent tracking on the last remaining survivor.
