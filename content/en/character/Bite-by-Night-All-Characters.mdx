### The Customer: The Ultimate Survivalist
The Customer is the starter character and serves as a reliable solo-queue option. Their kit is almost entirely selfish, focusing on keeping themselves alive and mobile.

*   **Pizza:** This ability heals the user for 20% of their current HP. Because it is percentage-based, it is most effective when your health is low. However, it features a short windup where you cannot run, making it dangerous to use during a chase.
*   **Drink:** A powerful utility tool that provides a 5-second speed boost and 15 seconds of the "Energetic" buff, which significantly reduces stamina drain. Be cautious: once the buff ends, you are hit with a 3-second slowness penalty.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Drink ability *during* a chase to gain distance, but ensure you have a safe loop or hiding spot ready for when the slowness debuff kicks in.

### The Medic: Essential Team Support
Currently considered the strongest survivor in the game, the Medic is a must-have for any serious team composition. Their ability to grant "Overhealth" changes the math of killer encounters.

*   **Heal:** This can restore up to 50 health to a teammate at a rate of 5 HP per second. Most importantly, it can "overheal" players up to 25 HP beyond their maximum. This extra health acts as a buffer that can completely negate a high-damage attack from killers like Springtrap.
*   **Self-Heal:** A more consistent version of the Customer's pizza, restoring 20-40 health. It cannot heal you past 74 HP, but it provides a much-needed safety net for the healer.

## The Sentinel Struggle: Why the Fighter Ranks Low

While the **Bite by Night All Characters** list includes the Fighter as a "Sentinel" meant to protect others, they currently struggle in the 2026 meta. The Fighter relies on a parry-and-stun mechanic that is extremely punishing if timed incorrectly.

The Fighter's **Parry** ability puts them in a stance to stun the killer for 1.5 seconds. Successful parries grant charges for the **Swing** ability, which can stun the killer for up to 3 seconds. However, several factors make this character difficult to use:

1.  **Low Health Pool:** Unlike similar characters in games like *Forsaken*, the Fighter has the same base health as a standard survivor.
2.  **High Killer Damage:** Most killers deal between 36 and 42 damage per hit. Missing a single parry often leads to a quick death.
3.  **Frame-Perfect Timing:** Some killers have an M1 (basic attack) windup as short as 2/60 frames. This requires a reaction time of 1/30th of a second, which is nearly impossible for the average player.
4.  **Weakness Debuff:** If you miss a parry, you are inflicted with "Weakness," making subsequent hits even more lethal.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to parry killers in dark areas of the map. The visual cues for their attack animations are nearly invisible, making a successful counter-attack highly unlikely.

## The Security Guard: Intelligence and Control

The Security Guard offers a unique playstyle focused on information gathering. While not as strong in a chase as the Customer, their ability to provide map-wide intel is invaluable for coordinated teams.

*   **Taser:** This ability slows the killer. The longer you charge the taser, the more significant the slow effect. However, you are slowed to a walking pace while charging, meaning this is better used to help a teammate escape rather than for self-defense.
*   **Security Cameras:** This allows the player to cycle through map cameras. Clicking on the killer or objectives like generators will highlight them for the entire team, allowing for better rotations and safer objective play.

## Advanced Strategies for Team Composition

When selecting from the **Bite by Night All Characters** roster, team synergy is more important than individual skill. A balanced team usually consists of:

*   **One Medic:** To maintain health and provide the Overhealth buffer.
*   **One Security Guard:** To keep the killer's location visible at all times.
*   **Two Customers:** To focus on fast generator completion and distracting the killer with high-mobility loops.

Avoid stacking multiple Fighters, as their lack of hit priority and fragile health makes them a liability in the current state of the game. Instead, focus on characters that can reduce the round timer by completing objectives while staying out of the killer's reach.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best character for beginners in Bite by Night?**

A: The **Customer** is the best choice for new players. Their abilities are straightforward and focus on self-survival, allowing you to learn the map layouts and killer patterns without relying too heavily on teammates.

**Q: How does the Overhealth mechanic work?**

A: Overhealth is provided by the Medic's heal ability. It adds up to 25 HP above your maximum health. This special health can "tank" a full hit from a killer; for example, a 36-damage hit from Springtrap will be completely absorbed by the 25 Overhealth points, leaving your base HP untouched.

**Q: Is the Fighter character getting a buff?**

A: While the developers have not officially announced specific changes, the community consensus is that the Fighter needs either more base health or a more forgiving parry window to be viable against the current **Bite by Night All Characters** roster.

**Q: Can the Security Guard see the killer at all times?**

A: Only if the killer is within the line of sight of one of the map's security cameras. The Security Guard must actively use the camera ability and click on the killer to highlight them for the rest of the team.
