## The Origin and Design of Ennard

Ennard is not a single entity but a collective hive-mind of the Funtime animatronics. In the lore of *Bite by Night*, they represent the ultimate goal of the scrapped machines: survival through any means necessary. By combining the parts of Circus Baby, Ballora, Funtime Freddy, and Funtime Foxy, Ennard becomes a humanoid mass of tangled silver wires, adorned with a haunting white clown mask.

Visually, the character is one of the most striking in the game. Their body is covered in various eyes—remnants of the animatronics that form them—which serve as a constant reminder of their "all-in-one" nature. In the 2026 version of the game, these eyes emit a faint white glow, making them a terrifying sight in low-light environments.

## Bite by Night Ennard: Ability Breakdown

The kit for **Bite by Night Ennard** is designed for players who enjoy "mind games" and tactical positioning. Unlike high-mobility killers like Springtrap, Ennard relies on trickery and dragging survivors into unfavorable positions.

### Hijacking (Deception Mechanic)
This is Ennard’s signature ability. It allows the killer to enter the corpse of a deceased player and "wear" them. While disguised, Ennard can walk among the survivors without a traditional Chase Theme playing. This creates a sense of paranoia, as players must constantly verify if their teammates are truly who they seem.

*   **Sluggish Movement:** While disguised, your walking speed is reduced.
*   **The Burst:** When ready to strike, Ennard can burst out of the corpse, damaging any survivors in the immediate vicinity.
*   **Tactical Use:** Use this to guard objectives or wait for a survivor to attempt a rescue before revealing your true form.

### Pull (Crowd Control)
Ennard can throw an extended wire in a straight line. If this wire connects with a survivor, it functions as a tether, dragging them directly into Ennard’s melee (M1) range. This is particularly effective for catching survivors who are attempting to loop around obstacles or vault through windows.

### Wirething (Pressure Tool)
By releasing a "Wirething" from their knee, Ennard can deploy an autonomous trap. These small wire entities follow survivors and explode upon contact, dealing significant damage. Survivors must "shake" their screen to remove the Wirething, which creates a distraction and prevents them from focusing on objectives.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Pull ability immediately after a survivor fails a skill check or is slowed by a Wirething to guarantee a successful hit.

## Advanced Strategies for Ennard Players

To excel as **Bite by Night Ennard**, you must learn to balance your two states: the disguised "Infiltrator" and the aggressive "Amalgamation." 

1.  **The Psychology of the Hijack:** Do not simply run at survivors while disguised. Instead, mimic the behavior of a player working on an objective. Wait for a second survivor to approach you before using the "Burst" mechanic to maximize damage.
2.  **Tethering through Windows:** The Pull ability is your best tool against "loopers." If a survivor is about to vault, time your wire throw to catch them mid-animation. This cancels their momentum and brings them back to your side of the wall.
3.  **Managing Wirethings:** These traps are best used in narrow corridors or near high-priority objectives. Because they require the survivor to shake their screen, they effectively "blind" the survivor for a few seconds, making them easy targets for a follow-up Pull.

## Countering Ennard as a Survivor

Facing **Bite by Night Ennard** can be overwhelming due to the lack of audio cues during their Hijacking phase. However, there are several ways to identify the killer before they strike.

*   **Watch for Sluggishness:** If a "teammate" is moving slower than usual or isn't sprinting when they should be, it is likely Ennard in disguise.
*   **Communication is Key:** If you are playing in a group, verify the status of your teammates. If someone is supposedly dead but walking toward you, run immediately.
*   **The Shake Mechanic:** If a Wirething latches onto you, prioritize finding a safe spot before shaking your screen. You are vulnerable during the removal process.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never heal a teammate near a corpse unless you have confirmed the killer's location. Ennard often waits inside bodies near popular healing spots.

## Lore and Trivia

The inclusion of Ennard in the *Bite by Night* roster is a nod to the deep [Five Nights at Freddy's lore](https://store.steampowered.com/app/506610/Five_Nights_at_Freddys_Sister_Location/) established in the 2016 Scott Cawthon classic. In the original story, Ennard successfully used the "Scooper" to remove Michael Afton's insides, allowing the amalgamation to use his skin as a suit to escape the underground facility. 

In the game, this is translated into the Hijacking mechanic, making Ennard the only killer capable of literal identity theft. Interestingly, Ennard was the final killer to be completed for the 2026 launch, as the developers worked to perfect the "burst" animation to ensure it felt impactful and fair for both sides.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Bite by Night Ennard available at the start of the game?**

A: Yes, Ennard is a confirmed launch-day killer. You do not need to wait for DLC or updates to play as this character in the 2026 release.

**Q: Can survivors hear a heartbeat when Ennard is disguised?**

A: No. One of the primary advantages of the Hijacking ability is that it suppresses Ennard's Chase Theme and heartbeat, making them a true stealth threat.

**Q: How do I remove the Wirething trap?**

A: When a Wirething attaches to your character, a progress bar will appear. You must shake your mouse or joystick until the bar is full to successfully remove the trap and avoid the explosion.

**Q: Can Ennard use the Pull ability while disguised?**

A: Generally, Ennard must reveal their true form to use mechanical abilities like Pull or Wirething. Attacking while disguised usually triggers the "Surprise" animation or the "Burst" mechanic.
