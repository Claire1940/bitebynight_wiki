## Understanding Springtrap’s Kit in Bite by Night

Springtrap is classified as a rushdown/trapper hybrid. This means he excels at both setting up "kill zones" and chasing down survivors once they are flushed out of hiding. Unlike pure trappers who might struggle in an open chase, Springtrap has the mobility to close gaps quickly, provided you manage his 70 stamina points effectively.

| Statistic | Value | Tactical Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Walk Speed** | 14 | Standard patrol speed for searching rooms. |
| **Run Speed** | 24 | High burst speed for closing distance in loops. |
| **Stamina** | 70 | Requires careful management during long chases. |
| **Health (HP)** | 1,750 | Allows for aggressive play even against defensive survivors. |

Springtrap begins the match with his axe equipped, providing a generous hitbox for basic attacks. However, his true potential is unlocked when he discards his weapon to access his secondary abilities.

## The Remnant Cleaver: Unlocking the Scream Moveset

The core mechanic of Springtrap revolves around the **Remnant Cleaver**. By aiming and throwing your axe, you deal immediate damage and inflict a 15-second bleeding status effect. More importantly, throwing the axe completely changes your moveset. While your basic attack damage decreases when using your fists, you gain access to the Scream and Charge abilities.

> **Warning:** Once the axe is thrown, it remains on the ground for 30 seconds before respawning in your hand. You can manually retrieve it sooner to regain your higher melee damage, but doing so will disable your Scream ability until the axe is thrown again.

## Detailed Bite by Night Springtrap Scream Guide: Mechanics and Effects

The Scream is arguably the most versatile tool in Springtrap’s arsenal. When activated, Springtrap stands still for approximately one second before releasing a deafening roar. This move serves three distinct purposes: crowd control, resource management, and intelligence gathering.

1.  **Blindness Induction:** Survivors caught within the radius of the scream suffer from blindness. The duration and intensity of this effect depend on their proximity to Springtrap.
2.  **Stamina Regeneration:** This is a passive benefit often overlooked by new players. Using the Scream instantly begins regenerating your stamina, allowing you to transition directly into a high-speed chase.
3.  **Aura Revelation:** The Scream reveals the auras of all nearby survivors. This is vital for finding players who are hiding behind cover or attempting to "ninja" a nearby objective.

## Combo Strategies: Scream and Charge Synergies

To maximize your efficiency, you should rarely use the Scream in isolation. The most effective way to secure a kill is the **Scream-Charge Combo**. Because the Scream reveals auras and regens stamina, it sets the perfect stage for the Charge ability.

When you activate Charge, Springtrap stomps forward with increased momentum. If you collide with a survivor during this rush, a unique cutscene plays where Springtrap screams directly into their face before tossing them aside. This move deals a flat **30% of the survivor's maximum HP**, making it one of the most consistent ways to whittle down healthy targets.

### The Execution Sequence
1.  **Throw Remnant Cleaver:** Hit the survivor to apply Bleeding.
2.  **Activate Scream:** Reveal their escape route and refill your stamina bar.
3.  **Initiate Charge:** Use the revealed aura to steer your charge directly into the blinded survivor.
4.  **Finish with Fists:** After the throw animation, use your 28-damage punches to close out the kill.

> **Tip:** If a survivor is looping you at a strong window or pallet, use the Scream to blind them. This often causes them to miss the vault timing or run into a wall, giving you an easy opening for a Charge.

## Map Control: Using Bear Traps and Auras

While the rushdown aspect of Springtrap is exciting, you cannot ignore his trapper roots. You can have up to **three Bear Traps** active on the map at any time. These traps are your primary source of area control, especially on maps with tight choke points.

| Trap Placement Tier | Location Type | Why it Works |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Priority** | Doorways/Thresholds | Survivors often sprint through doors without looking down. |
| **Medium Priority** | Top of Stairs | The camera angle makes it difficult to spot traps at the peak of a climb. |
| **Situational** | Objective Proxies | Forces survivors to choose between the objective and safety. |

A survivor caught in a Bear Trap is stunned for **10 seconds**. This is an eternity in *Bite by Night*. If you see a trap trigger notification, you should immediately head toward that location. If you have already thrown your axe, use the Scream as you approach to ensure no other survivors are nearby to help their teammate.

## Stamina Management and High-Level Play

The biggest hurdle for Springtrap players is his relatively low stamina pool of 70. Compared to other killers, Springtrap can run out of breath quickly if you hold the sprint button constantly. High-level play involves "feathering" your sprint and utilizing the Scream's regeneration properties to stay mobile.

You should play Springtrap similarly to the Slasher from *Forsaken*. Focus on short, explosive bursts of speed rather than long-distance marathons. If a chase is taking too long and you are low on stamina, it is often better to break off, throw your cleaver to reset your moveset, and use a Scream to reset your resources.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get my axe back after throwing the Remnant Cleaver?**

A: The axe will automatically respawn in your hand after 30 seconds. However, you can walk to the location where it landed and interact with it to pick it up instantly. Picking it up will revert your moveset to the default Swing and Bear Trap configuration.

**Q: Does the Scream ability work through walls?**

A: Yes, the aura revelation and the blindness effect of the Scream work through solid objects. This makes it an excellent tool for "wall-hacking" survivors who are trying to hide in lockers or behind large crates.

**Q: Why should I follow a bite by night springtrap scream guide instead of just using the axe?**

A: While the axe deals more raw damage per hit, the Scream moveset provides utility that the axe lacks. The Scream allows you to find hidden survivors and regenerate stamina, which is essential for maintaining pressure against experienced teams who know how to hide effectively.

**Q: Can survivors disable my Bear Traps?**

A: Survivors can attempt to disarm traps, but it takes time and leaves them vulnerable. Most survivors will try to simply jump over them or path around them, which is why placing them in narrow hallways or behind doors is the most effective strategy.
