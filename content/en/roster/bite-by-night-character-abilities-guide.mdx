## Killer Animatronic Abilities and Tactics

As an Animatronic, your primary objective is to prevent survivors from repairing generators and to eliminate them before the clock strikes 6:00 AM. Each killer has a unique kit designed for different playstyles, ranging from area denial to psychological warfare.

### The Rotten (Springtrap)
The Rotten is the quintessential beginner killer, offering a balanced kit that excels at both melee combat and area control. His ability to place traps makes him a nightmare in tight corridors.

| Ability | Keybind | Effect | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Axe Swing** | M1 (Left Click) | Lunges forward and deals **28 damage**. | Has a 2-second cooldown; do not spam. |
| **Axe Throw** | Q | Throws the axe to deal damage and **Bleed**. | You must manually pick up the axe after throwing. |
| **Bear Trap** | E | Places a trap that roots players for **5 seconds**. | Traps despawn after a set duration; replace them often. |

> **💡 Expert Tip:** Use Bear Traps at "choke points" or near active generators. If a survivor is caught, they are an easy target for a follow-up Axe Throw to initiate the Bleed status effect.

### The Mistake and The Doppelganger
While The Rotten focuses on physical traps, other killers utilize mobility and deception. The Mistake is known for its teleportation capabilities, allowing it to bypass survivor barricades and appear behind groups fixing generators. 

The Doppelganger, conversely, relies on the "Mimic" mechanic. This killer can assume the appearance of a dead player, tricking unsuspecting survivors into thinking they are meeting up with an ally. In the 2026 version of the game, seasoned players often check the player list to see who is officially "dead" to counter this, but it remains highly effective against uncoordinated teams.

## Survivor Role and Class Abilities

Survivors are divided into several classes, each with distinct utility. While the Customer is free for all players, specialized roles like the Medic or Security Guard must be purchased using Scrap, the in-game currency earned through successful matches.

### Comprehensive Survivor Ability Table

| Class | Ability 1 | Ability 2 | Role Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Customer** | **Pizza**: Heals 20% HP | **Drink**: Stamina Buff | Survival and basic objective play. |
| **Medic** | **Self-Heal**: Restores full HP | **Team-Heal**: Heals allies | Essential for long-term survival. |
| **Fighter** | **Axe Swing**: Stuns Killer | **Parry**: Blocks attacks | Aggressive defense and distraction. |
| **Security Guard** | **Cams**: Remote scouting | **Taser**: Ranged stun | Intelligence gathering and kiting. |

### The Medic: The Team's Lifeline
The Medic is arguably the most valuable role for high-level play. Their ability to restore full health to themselves and others negates the "Bleed" damage inflicted by killers like The Rotten. When playing as a Medic, you should prioritize staying hidden and only revealing yourself to assist a teammate who has just escaped a chase.

### The Security Guard and Fighter: Defensive Powerhouses
For players who prefer to engage the killer directly, the Fighter and Security Guard are the top choices. The Fighter can parry incoming attacks, which is vital when the killer is camping the exit gates. The Security Guard uses a taser to create distance, providing a window for teammates to finish the final generator repairs.

## Essential Gameplay Mechanics

Understanding the **bite by night character abilities guide** is only half the battle. You must also master the environment and technical settings to perform at your peak.

### Barricading and Map Control
Any survivor can barricade doors, but doing so requires completing a mini-game. You must keep a moving cursor within a larger circle to successfully lock the door. 
*   **Killers** can break through these, but it takes time.
*   **Survivors** can also be locked out by their own team, so communication is key.

### Optimized Settings for 2026
To improve your reaction time, follow these configuration steps in the settings menu:
1.  **Turn off Camera Shake:** This prevents your screen from wobbling during chases, allowing for better visibility.
2.  **Enable Performance Mode:** This displays your ping. High ping can make the Fighter's parry or the Rotten's Axe Throw nearly impossible to time.
3.  **Toggle Shift Lock:** Essential for killers to aim their lunges and for survivors to navigate tight corners while looking backward.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not sprint unnecessarily. Stamina is a finite resource, and if you are caught with an empty bar when the killer appears, you will not have the speed to reach a barricade or a hiding spot.

## How to Earn Scrap Fast
Scrap is the lifeblood of progression in Bite by Night. You need it to unlock the more powerful roles mentioned in this **bite by night character abilities guide**.

*   **As a Killer:** Focus on "Switching Targets." Don't tunnel one survivor for too long if others are repairing generators. Every kill and every generator regressed grants scrap.
*   **As a Survivor:** Repairing generators is the most consistent income source. Even if you don't escape, contributing to the objective ensures a decent payout at the end of the round.
*   **Survival Bonus:** Reaching 6:00 AM provides a massive multiplier to your total earnings for the match.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best character for a total beginner in Bite by Night?**

A: For survivors, the **Customer** is best as it is free and provides simple healing. For killers, **The Rotten** is highly recommended because his bear traps provide passive area control even if your aim with the axe isn't perfect yet.

**Q: How do I counter the Doppelganger's mimic ability?**

A: Always keep an eye on the player list at the top of the screen. If you see a "teammate" approaching you but their name is listed as dead or disconnected in the UI, it is the Doppelganger. This is a crucial tip often omitted in a basic **bite by night character abilities guide**.

**Q: Can the Medic heal through the Bleed effect?**

A: Yes, the Medic's heal ability will restore the health lost to Bleed and stop the lingering damage effect immediately. This makes the Medic a direct counter to The Rotten's axe throw.

**Q: Does the Fighter's parry work against all killer attacks?**

A: The parry works against standard M1 melee attacks. However, it does not protect you from being caught in a Bear Trap or from certain environmental hazards. Timing is vital, as a missed parry leaves you vulnerable for a short duration.

**Q: How long do the Security Guard's cameras last?**

A: Cameras are permanent until the killer finds and destroys them. As a Security Guard, hide your cameras in high-up corners or behind foliage to keep the killer highlighted for as long as possible.
