## Springtrap: The Apex Predator

Springtrap is widely considered the most well-rounded of the **Bite by Night Killers**. His kit provides a solution for almost every situation a Killer might face, from long-distance runners to survivors who try to "loop" the Killer around obstacles.

### Ability Breakdown
*   **Skillshot:** A ranged attack that allows Springtrap to finish off survivors who have gained too much distance. This is crucial for stopping escapes near the exit gates.
*   **Traps:** These can be placed in tight chokepoints or near objectives. Traps effectively disable a survivor’s ability to loop, forcing them into open areas where they are vulnerable.
*   **Shout:** A powerful utility move used for finding hidden players. The shout reveals survivor locations within a certain radius, making it nearly impossible for technicians to hide for long.
*   **Charge:** This provides Springtrap with some of the best mid-fight mobility in the game, allowing him to close the gap instantly during a chase.

Because of this mix of offense and scouting, Springtrap is the most reliable choice for players who want to maintain constant pressure on the map.

## Ennard: The Master of Deception

Ennard offers a completely different experience compared to the brute force of Springtrap. As one of the most unique **Bite by Night Killers**, Ennard relies on the "Disguise" mechanic to get close to survivors without triggering their internal alarms.

### Tactical Playstyle
Ennard’s power comes from his ability to look like a survivor. While experienced players might notice that Ennard moves slightly slower than a standard technician, the disguise is often enough to pull off devastating group attacks. Imagine four survivors working on a generator, only for one of them to suddenly transform into a mass of wires and metal.

Aside from deception, Ennard utilizes **Wirethings** for scouting and a **Pull** mechanic for mobility. The pull allows Ennard to navigate the verticality of the map or drag a fleeing survivor back into striking range. While he requires more finesse than Springtrap, his potential for chaos is unmatched in public lobbies where communication is low.

## Essential Killer Strategies for 2026

To succeed as a Killer in Bite by Night, you must do more than just chase. You need to manage the map and understand the survivor classes you are up against.

1.  **Prioritize the Medic:** If the survivor team has a Medic, they can undo all your hard work by healing injured teammates. Target the Medic early to reduce the team's overall sustain.
2.  **Monitor the Generators:** Survivors win by completing tasks. Use your mobility (like Springtrap's Charge or Ennard's Pull) to patrol objectives frequently.
3.  **Use the Environment:** Many maps have chokepoints. If you are playing Springtrap, always keep your traps active in high-traffic areas like doorways or narrow hallways.
4.  **Listen for Audio Cues:** The game features heavy emphasis on sound. Footsteps, panting, and the sparks of a failing generator are all clues to a survivor's location.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when chasing a "Customer" class survivor. Their "Drink" ability allows them to loop you for long periods, potentially distracting you while the rest of the team finishes the objectives.

## FAQ

**Q: Which Killer is best for beginners in Bite by Night?**

A: Springtrap is the best choice for beginners. His kit is very intuitive, and his "Shout" ability helps new players find survivors who are good at hiding.

**Q: Can survivors fight back against the Killers?**

A: While survivors cannot "kill" the animatronic, certain classes like the Fighter can stun or distract the Killer to buy time for their teammates.

**Q: How many Killers are available at launch?**

A: There are three Killers available at the March 28, 2026 launch: Springtrap, Ennard, and The Mimic. More characters like Marionette and Mangle are confirmed for future updates.

**Q: Does The Mimic have different gameplay modes?**

A: Yes, The Mimic features three distinct modes that players can swap between to adapt to different situations during a match, making it one of the most technical **Bite by Night Killers** to master.
