## The Customer: The Solo Specialist

The Customer (formerly known as the Default class) is the most self-centered role among the **Bite by Night Survivors**. This class is built for players who prefer to work independently or find themselves in lobbies with uncoordinated teammates.

The Customer starts with two consumable items:
1.  **Pizza**: Provides a significant HP heal, allowing you to recover after a close encounter without needing a Medic.
2.  **Soda**: Grants a temporary speed and stamina boost, which is vital for looping the killer around obstacles or making a break for a distant generator.

Because the Customer can sustain themselves and escape chases more effectively than other roles, they are often ranked as S-Tier for solo carry potential. However, their team utility is lower, usually limited to baiting the killer away from objectives while teammates repair generators.

> **💡 Tip:** Use your Soda specifically when you see the killer start their charge or when you are entering a dead-end area to quickly reposition.

## The Medic: Essential Team Support

The Medic is the backbone of any coordinated survival attempt. Equipped with a medkit styled after Freddy Fazbear, this class can heal both themselves and other **Bite by Night Survivors**.

The healing mechanic requires the Medic to hold down the action button while near a teammate. It restores approximately 5 HP per second. This is especially potent when paired with the Fighter class, as the Medic can keep the Fighter healthy while they attempt to parry the killer's strikes. In the 2026 meta, a team without at least one Medic rarely survives past 3:00 AM against high-skill killers like Springtrap.

## The Fighter: Combat and Weakness Stacking

For players who want to take a more active role in resisting the animatronics, the Fighter is the premier choice. Armed with a Fire Axe, the Fighter is the only class that can directly confront the killer to protect the team.

*   **Parry (E Key)**: Allows the Fighter to block incoming attacks if timed correctly.
*   **Counter Stacks**: Every successful counter builds a stack. When the Fighter manages to land a hit on the killer with their axe, it applies a **Weakness Effect**. The strength and duration of this debuff depend on the number of counter stacks accumulated.
*   **Tactical Use**: The Fighter should act as a bodyguard for the Security Guard or Medic while they work on generators.

## The Security Guard: The Tactical Leader

The Security Guard is widely considered the most balanced of the **Bite by Night Survivors**. This class provides unparalleled situational awareness through the camera system and a defensive tool in the form of the Taser.

### Taser Mechanics
The Taser is a chargeable weapon. If you hold the trigger too long, it can overcharge and fire randomly or drain battery inefficiently. However, a properly timed shot—ideally when the charge meter reaches the red zone—will stun the killer for several seconds, allowing your team to finish a nearby generator or escape a tight corridor.

### Camera System
The Security Guard can access a camera feed to scout the facility. By clicking on the killer or specific generators within the camera view, the Guard reveals their locations to all other survivors. This "ping" system is vital for keeping the team informed, though using the cameras does drain the item's battery over time.

## Survival Strategy: The 2026 Meta

To win a match in Bite by Night, survivors must activate a set number of generators (usually five to seven depending on the map) to accelerate the clock toward 6:00 AM. 

### Repairing Generators
Repairing a generator is a multi-step process that requires focus. If you are interrupted by the killer, you lose progress.

| Step | Action | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Connect Wires | Match the colored wires to their corresponding terminals. |
| **2** | Toggle Switches | Flip all inactive switches to the "On" position. |
| **3** | Prime Motor | Pull the starter handle (requires 4 successful pulls for a solo player). |
| **4** | Final Activation | The generator turns on, lighting up the immediate area. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Turning on a generator creates noise and light, which can attract the killer. Always have an escape route planned before you finish the final pull.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I escape once it hits 6:00 AM?**

A: Once the clock hits 6:00 AM, the main exit (usually near the elevator or entrance lobby) will open automatically. You must physically reach this zone to trigger the escape. Be careful, as many killers will camp the exit once the time approaches.

**Q: Can the Fighter actually kill the animatronic?**

A: No. The Fighter can stun, parry, and weaken the killer, but the animatronics cannot be permanently defeated. Your goal is always to delay the killer and complete objectives, not to win a fight to the death.

**Q: Does the Medic's healing medkit run out of charge?**

A: Yes, the medkit has a limited amount of healing "points" (often around 200 HP total). Once exhausted, the Medic becomes a standard survivor without their primary utility, so use your heals wisely on high-value targets like the Fighter or players with high generator progress.

**Q: What is the "Last Man Standing" mechanic?**

A: When only one of the **Bite by Night Survivors** remains in the game, the "Last Man Standing" buff may activate, providing a slight boost to stamina or repair speed to give the final player a desperate chance at reaching 6:00 AM.
