## Detailed Breakdown: The Forest

The Forest is perhaps the most atmospheric entry on the **bite by night all maps list**. It utilizes a combination of low-light conditions and organic obstacles to create a sense of constant dread. Unlike structured indoor maps, the Forest lacks clear "rooms," forcing survivors to rely on landmarks like large rocks, abandoned campsites, and specific tree clusters to orient themselves.

Navigating the Forest requires a keen ear. Because the ground is covered in dried leaves and twigs, movement noise is amplified. If you are using a high-mobility class, the Forest provides ample space to outrun killers, but the lack of hard corners makes it difficult to "break line of sight" once a chase has initiated.

> **Warning:** Avoid the center of the Forest during the late-game phase. The lack of hard cover makes you an easy target for killers with long-range detection abilities.

### Tactical Overview for The Forest
*   **Stealth:** Use the thickest bushes to crouch-walk. The dark green textures of skins like Ennard can blend surprisingly well into the shadows here.
*   **Objectives:** Generators and tasks are often spread far apart. It is recommended to work in pairs to ensure one player can distract the killer while the other completes the objective.
*   **Escape Routes:** Look for the illuminated gate markers. In the Forest, these are often the only sources of bright light, making them easy to find but dangerous to approach.

## Detailed Breakdown: Warehouse

The Warehouse is a stark contrast to the wilderness, offering a multi-layered industrial playground. As a staple of the **bite by night all maps list**, the Warehouse favors players who understand "looping"—the act of leading a killer around obstacles to waste their time. With its concrete walls and metal shelving, this map provides plenty of "hard cover" that completely obscures a survivor's silhouette.

The Warehouse is divided into several zones, including the loading docks, the central storage floor, and the upper catwalks. Verticality is the name of the game here. Survivors can often jump from the second floor to the first to create distance, though falling from too great a height may cause a temporary stagger.
