## How to Counter Rust Traps Effectively

Survival in *Bite By Night* depends on your ability to predict killer movement and trap placement. Since the **bite by night rust trap** is often placed in "choke points," you should always have an alternative escape route planned. 

1.  **Flashlight Scanning**: Use your flashlight to sweep the floor before entering a new room. Rust traps have a slight metallic glint that is visible under direct light.
2.  **Audio Cues**: Listen for a distinct "clinking" sound. This indicates a killer is currently placing a trap nearby.
3.  **Baiting the Trigger**: If you are healthy and have a teammate nearby, you can intentionally trigger a trap in a safe area to clear a path, though this is generally discouraged in solo play.
4.  **The "Alpha" Strategy**: Keep moving. A stationary survivor is an easy target for a killer like SpingTap, who can trap you in a corner before you even realize you're being hunted.

## FAQ

**Q: Can any killer use the bite by night rust trap?**

A: While most killers have access to some form of hazard, the specific "Rust Trap" variant is most commonly associated with the SpingTap killer and certain high-level animatronic builds. Standard killers may use different variations with shorter stun times.

**Q: How do I know if a trap has been placed near me?**

A: Listen for a mechanical "setting" sound. If you are playing against SpingTap, he may also yell phrases like "Don't you run from me!" or "I am the Alpha!" while placing traps to distract you.

**Q: Is it possible to destroy a bite by night rust trap?**

A: You cannot permanently destroy them, but you can disarm them by successfully parrying or by using certain character-specific items found in the map. Once triggered or disarmed, the trap usually enters a cooldown period before the killer can reset it.

**Q: Why do I get up slower after hitting a trap?**

A: This is part of the 2026 balance update. The "Rust" effect applies a temporary fatigue debuff to your character, simulating the physical toll of escaping a spring-loaded snare. You must wait for your stamina bar to replenish before you can sprint at full speed again.
