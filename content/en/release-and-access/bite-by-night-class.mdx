## S-Tier: The Medic

The Medic is widely considered the strongest **bite by night class** currently available. In a game where killers like the Mimic can deal 42 damage in a single swing, having a way to mitigate that damage is invaluable. The Medic doesn't just heal; they provide a buffer that can completely negate an incoming attack.

### Medic Abilities & Stats

*   **Heal (Active):** Heals a teammate for 5 HP per second, up to a total of 50 HP. This ability features a unique "Overheal" mechanic, allowing you to grant teammates up to 25 extra HP above their maximum.
*   **Self-Heal (Active):** Restores a random amount of health (between 20 and 40) to yourself. Note that this cannot exceed 74 HP and requires you to remain stationary.

The "Overhealth" provided by the Medic is the most powerful mechanic in the game. Even if a survivor only has 1 point of overhealth, it will completely absorb the damage of a single M1 attack from a killer. This makes the Medic a mandatory pick for high-level play.

## A-Tier: The Customer

The Customer is the "starter" survivor, but do not let that label fool you. This **bite by night class** excels at solo survival. While they offer very little in terms of direct team support, their ability to outrun and outlast the killer during a chase makes them a reliable choice for players who prefer to focus on objectives like generators.

### Customer Abilities & Stats

1.  **Pizza:** Heals 20% of your current HP. This is most effective when you are at high health, as the raw number of HP restored is higher, but it can provide a clutch save when you are low.
2.  **Drink:** Grants a 5-second speed boost and the "Energetic" buff for 15 seconds. Energetic significantly reduces stamina drain, allowing for extended loops.

The main drawback of the Customer is the 3-second slowness debuff that occurs after the Energetic boost ends. Timing this ability is crucial; if you are still in the killer's line of sight when the slowness hits, you are likely to be caught.

## B-Tier: The Security Guard

The Security Guard is a unique **bite by night class** that focuses on information gathering. In the dark, claustrophobic maps of Bite by Night, knowing the killer's location is often more valuable than having extra health.

### Security Guard Abilities & Stats

*   **Taser:** A projectile that slows the killer. The duration of the slow depends on how long you charge the shot. However, you are slowed to a walking pace while charging, making it risky to use during a close-range chase.
*   **Security Cameras:** Allows you to cycle through map cameras. Clicking on the killer or objectives will highlight them for the entire team.

The Security Guard works best when communicating with the team. By highlighting the killer, you allow your teammates to work on generators with confidence, knowing exactly which direction the threat is coming from.

## F-Tier: The Fighter (Sentinel)

Currently, the Fighter is the most difficult **bite by night class** to recommend. Designed as a "Sentinel" meant to protect others through stuns and parries, the Fighter suffers from the game's current balancing. Killers in Bite by Night have extremely fast attack windups, making the parry mechanic nearly impossible to land consistently.

As shown in the table above, the Fighter has a much lower margin for error than similar characters in other games like [Roblox Forsaken](https://www.roblox.com/games/13214532675/Forsaken). If you miss a parry, you are hit with a weakness debuff and likely downed in seconds due to the massive damage output of killers like Springtrap.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid playing the Fighter unless you have mastered the killer's attack animations. The 1/30th of a second reaction window is unforgiving for most players.

## Health and Damage Mechanics

Understanding the math behind survival is essential for every **bite by night class**. Because there is no traditional "downed" state where you can be revived easily, every hit you take brings you significantly closer to being removed from the round.

| Killer | M1 Damage | Hits to Kill (100 HP) |
|---|---|---|
| **Springtrap** | 36 | 3 Hits |
| **The Mimic** | 42 | 3 Hits |

Because most killers kill in exactly three hits, the Medic's ability to "tank" one hit with overhealth effectively increases a survivor's lifespan by 33%. This is why the Medic remains the pinnacle of the survivor meta in 2026.

## Survival Tips for All Classes

Regardless of which **bite by night class** you choose, these general strategies will help you survive the night:

*   **Manage Your Stamina:** Never let your stamina bar hit zero. If you are exhausted, you cannot sprint, making you an easy target for the killer's high-damage M1 attacks.
*   **Listen for Audio Cues:** Sound design is top-tier in this game. You can often hear the mechanical whirring or heavy footsteps of an animatronic long before you see them.
*   **Prioritize Generators:** The rounds are long and punishing. The faster you complete objectives, the less time the killer has to whittle down your team's resources.
*   **Safe Healing:** Most healing abilities (like the Medic's or Customer's) require you to slow down or stop. Always ensure you are behind a locked door or in a hidden corner before attempting to heal.

## FAQ

**Q: Which bite by night class is best for solo players?**

A: The Customer is the best choice for solo players. Their "Drink" ability allows them to escape chases independently without needing to rely on teammates for protection or healing.

**Q: Can the Medic heal themselves to full health?**

A: No. The Medic's self-heal ability is capped at 74 HP. To reach 100 HP or gain overhealth, you would need another Medic on the team to heal you.

**Q: Why is the Fighter considered so bad in the current meta?**

A: The Fighter struggles because the killer attack animations are incredibly fast (sometimes only 2 frames of windup), making parrying a matter of luck rather than skill. Additionally, the Fighter lacks the extra health needed to survive the high damage of current killers.

**Q: How do the Security Guard's cameras work?**

A: When using the camera ability, you enter a separate view where you can cycle through fixed locations on the map. You can "ping" the killer by clicking on them, which outlines their silhouette for your entire team for a short duration.
