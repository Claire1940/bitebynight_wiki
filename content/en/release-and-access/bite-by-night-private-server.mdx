## Managing Server Permissions and Invites

Once your **bite by night private server** is active, you need to know how to bring your friends into the fold. Roblox provides a robust set of tools for managing access, ensuring that your server remains a safe haven for your chosen group.

### Inviting Friends
There are two primary ways to allow others into your server:
1.  **Friends Allowed:** By toggling this setting in the configuration menu, anyone on your Roblox friends list can see your server in the "Servers" tab and join freely.
2.  **Server Link:** You can generate a unique URL. Anyone with this link can join the server, even if they are not on your friends list. This is ideal for Discord communities or temporary groups.

### Configuration Checklist
*   **Server Name:** Keep it recognizable.
*   **Allow Friends:** Toggle "On" for easy access.
*   **Member List:** Manually add users by their exact Roblox username if you don't want to open it to your entire friends list.

> **Tip:** If you are hosting a tournament or a specific event, use the "Server Link" method and reset the link once the desired players have joined to prevent unwanted guests from leaking the URL.

## Troubleshooting Common Connection Issues

Sometimes, your **bite by night private server** might not appear or might prevent friends from joining. This is usually due to platform-wide settings rather than a bug within the game itself.

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Server Not Visible** | Privacy Settings | Set "Who can join me in private servers?" to **Everyone** or **Friends** in Roblox settings. |
| **Link Not Working** | Expired Link | Generate a new server link in the configuration menu. |
| **Joining Failed** | Game Update | Ensure both the host and the joiner have updated their Roblox client. |
| **Lag/Latency** | Server Region | Roblox automatically assigns regions; try restarting the instance to get a better node. |
