## Overview of the Technician Class

The Technician is designed as a "trapper" survivor. Unlike the Fighter, who relies on parries, or the Medic, who focuses on sustain, the Technician uses advanced gadgets to manipulate the environment. Their primary goal is to waste the killer's time by creating "dead zones" where the killer is slowed or stunned, and by using phasing abilities to escape loops that would be fatal for any other class.

## Core Abilities: Static Field and Vanny Mask

The power of the **bite by night technician class** lies in its two active abilities. Mastering the "doohickeys" in your inventory is the difference between a successful escape and an early exit from the match.

### Static Field (Deployable Cube)
The Technician can deploy a small cube—often referred to by the community as a "dingle dongle"—that generates a static field. When a killer enters this radius, they are hit with a brief stun or a significant daze effect.

*   **Placement Strategy:** Always place these near high-traffic chokepoints, such as the doors in the Forest map or around tight corners.
*   **Counterplay:** Killers can destroy these traps using a basic M1 attack. To prevent this, place them in spots where the killer must commit to a lunge or a chase to reach them.
*   **Cooldown:** Note that the trap disappears once your cooldown resets, so timing your deployment is critical during a chase.

### The Vanny Mask (Phasing)
By pressing 'Q', the Technician dons the iconic Vanny Mask. This shifts the player into a first-person perspective and grants the ability to phase through certain solid objects and walls.

*   **Wall Phasing:** This allows you to cut through loops and enter rooms that the killer must navigate around. 
*   **Vision:** While the mask is active, you gain enhanced visibility, allowing you to track the killer through obstacles.
*   **The Trade-off:** Using the mask applies a **Slowness** debuff. You cannot simply outrun a killer while wearing it; you must use it to reach a specific destination or obstacle that the killer cannot pass through.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to phase through exterior map boundaries or certain "hard" walls. The game’s physics engine may reset your position, leaving you vulnerable to a killer's attack.

## Strategic Gameplay for the Bite By Night Technician Class

Playing the **bite by night technician class** requires a proactive mindset. You should not wait for the chase to begin before planning your escape route.

### Map Control and Trap Placement
Because the Static Field has a limited duration tied to your cooldown, you should treat it as a reactive tool during chases rather than a long-term trap like Springtrap's bear traps. If you are working on a generator, place the cube at the most likely entrance. If the killer approaches, the stun will give you the 2-3 seconds needed to reach a nearby pallet or window.

### Mastering the Vanny Mask Loop
The most effective way to use the Vanny Mask is the "Teleport Bait." 
1.  Lead the killer toward a thin wall.
2.  Activate the mask and phase through.
3.  The killer will likely try to walk around the wall to intercept you.
4.  Immediately phase back through to the original side.

This maneuver can completely negate the killer's momentum, especially against heavy hitters like the Mimic or Ennard.

|---|---|---|
| **vs. Springtrap** | Weakness | Phasing negates his axe throw trajectory. |
| **vs. Mimic** | Neutral | Mimic's underground tracking can still find you. |
| **vs. Ennard** | Strength | Static fields can disrupt Ennard's wonky grab hitboxes. |

## FAQ

**Q: Is the bite by night technician class better than the Fighter?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. The Fighter is better for direct confrontation and protecting teammates through parries. The Technician is superior for solo survival and "ghosting" the killer by phasing through walls and using traps to escape chases.

**Q: How do I unlock the Vanny Mask?**

A: The Vanny Mask is a core ability of the Technician class. Once you purchase or select the Technician class in the character menu, the mask is automatically assigned to your 'Q' keybind.

**Q: Can the killer destroy the Technician's static field cubes?**

A: Yes. Killers can break the cubes with a single M1 (basic attack). To maximize the value of your traps, place them in locations where the killer is forced to trigger the field before they can reach the cube to destroy it.

**Q: Does the Technician's phasing work on all maps?**

A: Yes, the phasing mechanic works on all current maps, including the Forest and the Pizzeria. However, it only works on specific internal walls and obstacles. You cannot phase through the map's outer boundaries.

**Q: Why do I get slowed down when using the Vanny Mask?**

A: The slowness is a balance mechanic. Without it, the Technician would be too powerful. The goal is to use the mask for tactical repositioning through walls, not for outrunning the killer in an open field.
