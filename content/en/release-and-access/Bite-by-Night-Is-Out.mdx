## Mastering the Survivor Classes

Survivors in Bite by Night are not just defenseless victims; they are divided into specialized classes that must work together to escape. Choosing the right composition for your team is vital for counteracting the killer's pressure.

### The Fighter
The Fighter is the team's primary line of defense. Equipped with a fire axe, this class can directly confront the killer. The Fighter can swing their axe to stun the killer or perform a parry to negate incoming damage and provide a brief opening for teammates to escape.

### The Healer (Medic)
Sustainability is the name of the game for the Healer. This class can restore the health of injured teammates. In a game where the killer can eliminate players in just a few hits, having a dedicated Medic to reset the team's HP can turn the tide of a losing match.

### The Security Guard
The Security Guard provides unparalleled utility through information and crowd control. They carry a taser that can be charged for a powerful ranged stun. Additionally, they can access map cameras to highlight the killer's location for the entire team, making it much harder for the animatronics to pull off stealth attacks.

### The Customer
Often viewed as the "Survivalist" or "Generalist" class, the Customer focuses on self-preservation and minor buffs. They can consume items like pizza to heal themselves or drink sodas to gain a temporary speed boost, though they must deal with a slowness debuff once the sugar rush wears off.

## The Killers: Iconic Animatronic Threats

Now that **Bite by Night Is Out**, the killer roster features some of the most terrifying entities from the FNAF franchise and its fan-made counterparts. Mastering these characters requires understanding their unique ability cycles and "Agony" management.

### Springtrap
Springtrap is a relentless hunter with a kit designed for area control and tracking. He can throw his axe for ranged damage, place bear traps to shut down popular looping spots, and let out a terrifying scream that reveals nearby survivors while regenerating his stamina. He is the most balanced killer for beginners.

### The Mimic
The Mimic is a complex, high-skill-cap killer that utilizes different "modes" to adapt to the situation. He can switch between Strength (high damage), Speed (high mobility), and Stealth (undetectable) modes. His ability to dig underground and reappear beneath survivors makes him a master of jumpscares.

### Ennard
Ennard specializes in deception. One of his most powerful traits is the ability to disguise himself as a survivor, allowing him to walk right up to generators without raising suspicion. He can also summon parts of himself to block paths or strike from a distance, making him a psychological threat as much as a physical one.

## Core Gameplay Mechanics and Objectives

To win as a survivor, you must survive until 6:00 AM. While the timer ticks down naturally, survivors can speed up the process by interacting with generators scattered across the map.

1.  **Generator Repair:** Generators require a "tug" mini-game where players must maintain their mouse or finger in a specific zone. Completing a generator significantly reduces the time remaining until dawn.
2.  **Stamina Management:** Both survivors and killers have stamina bars. Sprinting, jumping, and using abilities consume stamina. If a survivor runs out of stamina during a chase, they become an easy target.
3.  **Barricades and Doors:** Maps are filled with doors that can be slammed shut and barricaded. While these offer temporary safety, killers can eventually kick them down, permanently removing that obstacle from the map.
4.  **Last Man Standing:** If only one survivor remains, special mechanics may trigger, such as the opening of an emergency escape hatch or a final adrenaline boost to help them reach the exit gates.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when using barricades. If you slam a door on a teammate, you might trap them with the killer, leading to an early elimination for your team.

## Strategies for Survival

With **Bite by Night Is Out**, the meta is evolving quickly. Here are some essential strategies for both sides:

### For Survivors:
*   **Don't Group Up Too Much:** While it's tempting to stay together, one Ennard or Springtrap can easily wipe a group. Spread out to pressure multiple generators simultaneously.
*   **Save the Taser:** If you are a Security Guard, don't waste your taser on a killer who isn't chasing someone. Save it for "the save"—stunning the killer right as they are about to land a finishing blow on a teammate.
*   **Learn the Loops:** Every map has "loops"—areas with windows and pallets that can be used to keep the killer busy. A good Fighter can loop a killer for minutes, giving the rest of the team time to finish every generator.

### For Killers:
*   **Pressure the Generators:** Don't get tunnel-visioned on one survivor. if a chase is taking too long, break off and check your generators. Survivors win by doing objectives, not by running.
*   **Use Your Environment:** As Springtrap, place your bear traps in narrow hallways or behind doors. As The Mimic, use your digging ability to bypass walls and surprise survivors working on generators in corners.
*   **Manage Your Agony:** Your most powerful abilities often have long cooldowns or high costs. Don't use your reveal scream as Springtrap if you already know where everyone is.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Bite by Night free to play?**

A: Yes, Bite by Night is a free-to-play title on Roblox. However, players can choose to spend Robux on "Malice" to unlock certain killers or cosmetics faster, or donate to the developers to support future updates.

**Q: How many players can be in a single match?**

A: A standard match consists of five players: four survivors and one killer. There are also private server options for those who want to play with a specific group of friends.

**Q: Now that Bite by Night Is Out, will there be more killers added?**

A: Yes! The developers have already confirmed that characters like Ennard and potentially Monster Rat are part of the roadmap. The game is designed to be an evolving experience with seasonal content.

**Q: Can I play Bite by Night on mobile?**

A: Yes, the game is compatible with mobile devices. The developers have included a "Potato Mode" in the settings to help lower-end mobile devices run the game more smoothly.

**Q: What happens if I am the last survivor left?**

A: When you are the "Last Man Standing," your objective remains the same (survive until 6 AM), but you may receive certain buffs or have access to an emergency exit to balance the difficulty of being alone against the killer.
