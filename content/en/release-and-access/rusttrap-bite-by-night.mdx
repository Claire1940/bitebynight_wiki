## Killer Mechanics and Traps

The primary strength of the **rusttrap bite by night** gameplay style lies in the "Trap Range." ### Trap Placement Strategies
1. **Doorway Sniping:** Placing a trap immediately behind a door so that the interaction animation locks the survivor into the trap's hitboxes.
2. **Loop Denial:** Setting traps at the apex of a pallet loop, forcing the survivor to either take a hit or abandon the loop entirely.
3. **Extraction Camping:** While controversial, placing traps near final exit points remains a highly effective, albeit frustrating, tactic.

## Map-Specific Survival Strategies

Success in **rusttrap bite by night** depends heavily on your knowledge of the terrain. Each map offers different advantages for the Rusttrap's trapping abilities.

### The Forest Map
This map is notorious for the Mimic's wall-clipping glitch. Survivors should stay away from the map boundaries and the cluster of barrels near the center. Because the Forest is open, the Rusttrap can see you from a distance; use the dense foliage to break line-of-sight rather than relying on hard cover.

### The Warehouse
The Warehouse features several ramps and elevated platforms. While some players attempt to go AFK in these spots, the Rusttrap's ability to throw projectiles makes these "safe zones" death traps. Use the narrow aisles to your advantage by dropping pallets early to block the killer's movement.

### The Pizzeria
This is the most dangerous map for survivors due to the high density of corners. A **rusttrap bite by night** player can easily trap every exit of a room, leaving you with no escape. Always keep a "Parry" ready if the game mechanics allow, or ensure you have a clear view of the floor at all times.

## Countering the Ennard Insta-Kill

The Ennard (Doppelganger) killer previously possessed a glitch where its "E" ability would instantly down a survivor regardless of health. While the April 2026 patches have addressed the core of this issue, players with high latency (ping) may still experience "phantom hits" that result in an instant kill.

To counter this, survivors must maintain a "reaction gap." Do not let Ennard get within lunging distance. If you see the Ennard player stop moving for a split second, they are likely lining up an ability—this is your cue to zig-zag or use a stamina burst to change your trajectory.
