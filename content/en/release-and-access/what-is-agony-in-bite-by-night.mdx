## Understanding the Agony Mechanic

In the lore of the game, Agony represents the residual energy left behind by fallen survivors and the malevolent force driving the animatronics. Mechanically, it functions as a "prestige" currency. Unlike Scrap, which is awarded for almost every action—from fixing a generator to landing a single hit as a killer—Agony is typically reserved for significant milestones and high-performance metrics.

For survivors, Agony is a reward for bravery and efficiency. For killers, it is a measure of your lethality. In 2026, the developers updated the system to ensure that players cannot simply "AFK farm" this resource, making it one of the most respected markers of skill in the community.

## How to Earn Agony Effectively

To accumulate this resource, you must move beyond the basics of the "Beginner's Guide." While a standard match might net you 400 Scrap, Agony is awarded in much smaller increments, often 5 to 10 per successful high-tier action.

### 1. Achieving "Perfect" Wins
A "Perfect Win" for a killer involves eliminating all survivors before the 4:00 AM mark. For survivors, a perfect win requires all players to escape through the storage area exit without a single death. These feats trigger an Agony bonus at the post-match screen.

### 2. The Prestige System
Once you reach the maximum level for a specific role (such as the Security Guard or The Rotten), you have the option to "Prestige." Resetting your progress for that character rewards you with a significant lump sum of Agony, which can then be used to purchase the more expensive animatronics like The Mistake.

### 3. Killing High-Level Survivors
As an animatronic, targeting survivors who have a high "Bounty" (indicated by their performance in the current match) will grant a small amount of Agony upon their final elimination. This encourages killers to switch targets and go after the players who are most efficiently fixing generators.

## Spending Agony: The High-Tier Unlockables

The primary reason players grind for this resource is to access the advanced roster. While "The Rotten" (Springtrap) is an excellent starter killer with his bear traps and axe-throwing abilities, the Agony-exclusive killers offer unique gameplay loops.

### The Mistake and The Doppelganger
*   **The Mistake:** This animatronic relies on teleportation and psychological horror. It can bypass the standard "bottleneck" traps that survivors set at doors, making it a nightmare for players who rely on barricades.
*   **The Doppelganger:** This is perhaps the most dangerous role in the game. It allows the killer to shapeshift into a dead survivor. In a lobby full of uncoordinated players, the Doppelganger can walk right up to a generator, pretend to help, and then execute everyone in seconds.

### High-Tier Survivor Roles
While most survivors are bought with Scrap, certain "Veteran" variants of the Fighter and Security Guard roles require Agony. These variants often have reduced cooldowns on their tasers or parry abilities, providing a slight edge in high-stakes matches.

## Strategies for Agony Accumulation

If you are looking to maximize your gains, you should focus on the "Killer" role during the late-night server cycles. Because killing survivors grants Scrap and has a chance to drop Agony on "Final Blows," being an aggressive animatronic is generally faster than playing as a passive survivor.

1.  **Memorize the Map:** As a killer, knowing the storage area exit is vital. Camp the exit only when the clock hits 6:00 AM to secure those final kills that trigger the win bonus.
2.  **Use Traps Wisely:** If playing as The Rotten, place bear traps at choke points. Each survivor caught in a trap provides points that contribute to your end-of-match Agony calculation.
3.  **Don't Spam M1:** Every swing has a 2-second cooldown. Missing your hits lowers your "Accuracy" rating, which can negatively impact your resource rewards.
4.  **Target Generators:** Survivors gain the most points by fixing generators. By interrupting them, you prolong the match, giving you more opportunities to farm the necessary actions for Agony.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when using the "Doppelganger" ability. Advanced players often check the player list (Tab) to see who is actually dead. If you mimic someone who is clearly listed as deceased, you will be spotted immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: What is agony in bite by night compared to Scrap?**

A: Agony is a rare, prestige currency used for high-tier animatronics and elite skins, whereas Scrap is the common currency used for basic roles and starter killers. You earn Scrap through general play, but Agony requires winning matches or completing difficult challenges.

**Q: Can I buy Agony with Robux?**

A: While you can buy VIP passes and Scrap boosters, Agony is primarily designed as an "earned" currency to reward skilled play. However, certain limited-time bundles in the shop may occasionally include Agony as a bonus.

**Q: Does Agony reset every season?**

A: No, your accumulated Agony stays with your account across seasons. Only your competitive rank and certain seasonal event points reset, allowing you to save up for expensive killers over a long period.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Agony as a beginner?**

A: The fastest way is to play as "The Rotten" and focus on getting full-team wipes. Winning the game as a killer by eliminating all survivors provides the highest consistent payout of Agony for new players.
