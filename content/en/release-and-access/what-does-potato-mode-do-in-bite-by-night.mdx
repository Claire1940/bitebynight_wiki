## Understanding Potato Mode and Performance Settings

The "Potato Mode" toggle is located within the in-game settings menu. When enabled, it drastically reduces the rendering load on your CPU and GPU. This is achieved by lowering texture resolutions, removing non-essential environmental particles, and simplifying lighting effects. For players on mobile devices or "potato" PCs, this setting is often the difference between a playable 60 FPS and a slideshow that makes escaping killers impossible.

Beyond just the graphics, the game offers a "Performance Mode" which is distinct from Potato Mode. While Potato Mode handles the visuals, Performance Mode provides a diagnostic overlay. This allows you to monitor your ping and server age in real-time. In a game where timing your escapes and parries is everything, knowing your exact latency is a massive competitive advantage.

| Setting Name | Recommended Value | Impact on Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Potato Mode** | **ON** (Low-end) / **OFF** (High-end) | Increases FPS by reducing graphical fidelity. |
| **Performance Mode** | **ON** | Displays Ping and Server Age for better timing. |
| **Camera Shake** | **OFF** | Reduces visual clutter and prevents motion sickness. |
| **Shift Lock** | **ON** | Essential for aiming killer abilities and navigating. |
| **Show Barriers** | **ON** | Helps identify map boundaries and escape routes. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always turn off Camera Shake. While it adds to the "horror" atmosphere, it makes it significantly harder to track the killer's movement during a high-stakes chase.

## Survivor Roles and Survival Strategies

Once your performance is optimized, you need to choose a survivor role that fits your playstyle. Each role comes with unique items and abilities that can turn the tide of a match. As a beginner, focusing on roles that provide utility or healing is generally the most effective way to earn scrap and learn the map layouts.

The primary objective for survivors is to fix generators and submit batteries before 6:00 AM. Lag can make the skill checks on these generators much harder, which is another reason why knowing **what does potato mode do in bite by night** is so important for objective-focused players.

### Survivor Role Comparison

| Role | Primary Ability | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Customer** | Pizza (Heal) / Drink (Stamina) | Beginners / Solo Survival |
| **Medic** | Self-Heal / Team-Heal | Support-oriented players |
| **Fighter** | Axe Swing / Parry | Aggressive players who want to stun killers |
| **Security Guard** | Taser / Remote Cameras | Map awareness and crowd control |

The **Customer** role is the default free option, providing a balanced start. The Pizza ability heals 20% HP, while the Drink provides a temporary stamina buff. However, the **Medic** is often considered the best beginner-to-intermediate role because it allows for full HP recovery for both the player and their teammates. If you find yourself frequently trapped by the killer's bear traps, having a Medic nearby is a lifesaver.

## Playing as the Animatronic: The Rotten Strategy

The Rotten (often referred to by the community as Springtrap) is the standard killer available to most players. Mastering this character requires understanding his three core abilities: the M1 lunge, the Q axe throw, and the E bear trap. 

1.  **M1 Lunge:** A standard forward strike that deals approximately 28 damage.
2.  **Q Axe Throw:** Chunks a significant portion of health and applies a bleed effect. You must manually retrieve your axe after throwing it.
3.  **E Bear Trap:** Places a trap that snares survivors for roughly 5 seconds.

When playing as the Rotten, your goal is to prevent the survivors from completing the generators. If they finish the objectives, the clock skips forward, bringing the game closer to 6:00 AM and their eventual escape. You should place your bear traps at "choke points"—narrow hallways or doorways where survivors have no choice but to step through.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not spam your left-click (M1). There is a 2-second cooldown between swings. If you miss, you leave yourself vulnerable to being stunned by a Fighter or tased by a Security Guard.

## Advanced Tactics and Map Awareness

Winning in Bite by Night isn't just about fast reflexes; it's about knowledge. Survivors should prioritize memorizing generator locations and the final exit gate. On most maps, the exit is located in a specific "storage" or "loading dock" area. As the clock nears 6:00 AM, the killer will likely begin camping this area.

For survivors, the "Barricade" mechanic is a powerful but situational tool. By holding the interaction button on a door, you can create a barrier that neither killers nor other survivors can easily pass. This is perfect for buying time while a teammate finishes a nearby generator. However, be careful not to trap your own teammates in a dead end.

### How to Earn Scrap Efficiently
Scrap is the lifeblood of progression in Bite by Night. You use it to unlock new survivor roles and more powerful animatronics like The Mistake or The Doppelganger.

*   **As a Survivor:** You earn scrap by completing generator progress bars, submitting batteries, and surviving until the end of the round.
*   **As a Killer:** You earn scrap for every hit landed and every survivor eliminated. A high-kill game can net you upwards of 400 scrap in a single session.

## The Role of Sound and Stamina

Many players ignore the importance of audio cues. Bite by Night uses spatial audio to help you locate generators and, more importantly, the killer. If you hear heavy mechanical footsteps or the sound of an axe dragging, the killer is nearby. 

Stamina management is equally vital. You should avoid sprinting unless you are actively being chased or need to reach a generator quickly. If your stamina bar hits zero just as the killer finds you, you won't have the energy to dodge their axe throw or lunge. Using the **Customer's** drink ability can help, but it comes with a "stamina exhaustion" penalty once the effect wears off.

## FAQ

**Q: What does potato mode do in bite by night specifically for mobile players?**

A: For mobile players, Potato Mode is essential. It disables high-resolution shadows and complex shaders that typically cause mobile devices to overheat or crash. It ensures the game remains responsive even during intense chases with multiple players on screen.

**Q: Does Potato Mode give you a competitive advantage?**

A: In some ways, yes. By removing extra grass, particles, and fog, it can actually be easier to spot survivors or the killer from a distance. Many competitive players use "low" settings in various games to reduce visual noise, and Bite by Night is no different.

**Q: How do I unlock the Medic or Fighter roles?**

A: These roles are purchased in the in-game shop using Scrap. You can earn Scrap by playing matches as either a survivor or an animatronic. The Medic is highly recommended as your first purchase due to its high utility in team play.

**Q: Can the killer see me if I'm hiding in a room with a barricaded door?**

A: The killer cannot see through the door, but they can hear you if you are sprinting or performing actions. Furthermore, some killers like The Security Guard (if they were to be an animatronic variant) or characters with camera access can track your general location. Always stay quiet when a killer is nearby.
