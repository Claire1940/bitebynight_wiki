## Survivor Classes and Roles

On the **Bite by Night Release Date**, players will have access to four distinct survivor classes. Each class is designed to fulfill a specific role within the team, encouraging cooperation and strategic planning. Choosing the right composition can be the difference between a clean escape and a total team wipe.

### The Fighter
The Fighter is the team's primary line of defense. Equipped with a fire axe, this class is capable of stunning the killer and even parrying certain attacks. While the Fighter cannot kill the animatronics, they can buy precious seconds for their teammates to finish generators.

### The Healer (Medic)
The Healer is arguably the most vital support role. They possess the ability to "overheal" teammates, giving them a buffer against the killer's damage. In high-level play, two Healers can often rotate their abilities to keep the team alive through prolonged chases.

### The Security Guard
Armed with a taser, the Security Guard focuses on utility and surveillance. They can access camera systems to track the killer's movements and use their taser to disrupt a hunt. However, the taser has limited power and must be managed carefully.

### The Customer
While initially appearing as a "civilian" role, the Customer focuses on survival and resource consumption. They can interact with environmental items like pizza and soda to maintain their stamina and health, making them difficult for the killer to pin down over long periods.

## Confirmed Killers at Launch

The most exciting aspect of the **Bite by Night Release Date** is the inclusion of fan-favorite animatronics. Each killer features a unique move set and playstyle, requiring survivors to adapt their strategies on the fly.

### Springtrap
Originating from *FNAF 3*, Springtrap is a relentless hunter. He excels at setting traps and closing distances quickly. His presence is often signaled by mechanical whirring and heavy footsteps, making him a terrifying foe in tight corridors.

### The Mimic
The Mimic is a highly technical killer with two distinct move sets: Power and Stealth. In Stealth mode, he can navigate the map silently, often appearing right behind unsuspecting survivors. In Power mode, he utilizes a drill-like attack that can phase through certain environmental obstacles.

### Ennard
Ennard represents the "amalgamation" style of play. As a collection of Funtime animatronics, Ennard has high mobility and can often reach areas other killers cannot. His ability to "scoop" survivors makes him a high-priority threat that must be tracked via the Security Guard's cameras at all times.

## Gameplay Mechanics and Objectives

To win a match in *Bite by Night*, survivors must focus on repairing generators scattered across the map. These generators power the facility and are the only way to unlock the exit gates.

1.  **Locate Generators:** Use environmental cues and sound to find inactive generators.
2.  **Skill Checks:** While repairing, players must hit timed skill checks. Failing a check causes a loud explosion, alerting the killer to your location.
3.  **Manage Resources:** Batteries for tasers and charges for medkits are limited. Scavenge the map for refills.
4.  **Survive until 6:00 AM:** Once the objectives are met, the exit gates will power up. Survivors must reach these gates and hold the interaction button to escape.

## Tips for Surviving Your First Night

If you are jumping in on the **Bite by Night Release Date**, keep these strategies in mind to increase your chances of survival:

*   **Adjust Your Graphics:** If the maps feel too dark, lowering your graphics settings can sometimes improve visibility, making it easier to spot the killer from a distance.
*   **Listen for the Heartbeat:** As the killer approaches, your character's heartbeat will increase in volume. This is your cue to find a hiding spot or prepare a taser/axe.
*   **Don't Talk While Hiding:** If you are using proximity voice chat, remember that the killer can hear you. Stay silent when tucked away in a locker or behind a crate.
*   **Utilize First-Person Mode:** While third-person offers better peripheral vision, first-person mode can be more immersive and helpful for precise interactions with generators.
