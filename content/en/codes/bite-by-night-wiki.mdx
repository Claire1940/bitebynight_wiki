## Core Gameplay Objectives

Bite by Night pits a group of survivors against a single, powerful killer. The match is dictated by a clock system where survivors must complete specific objectives before the 6:00 AM mark to trigger the escape phase. Unlike some asymmetrical games that rely purely on a timer, progress in this game is tied to mechanical repairs and resource management.

### Survivor Goals
Survivors must locate and repair five generators scattered across the map. Each completed generator adds valuable time to the clock, delaying the inevitable end of the round and providing more breathing room to find the exit.

| Objective | Requirement | Reward/Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Generator Repair** | 5 Total | Adds time to the match clock |
| **Battery Installation** | Found in world | Powers specific gates or tools |
| **6:00 AM Phase** | Reach time limit | Unlocks the Exit Gates |
| **Escape** | Reach Exit Zone | **Victory** for the survivor |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep your camera moving while repairing. Killers can approach from multiple angles, and staying stationary without visual awareness is the fastest way to get caught.

## Survivor Items and Perks

Survivors aren't entirely defenseless. Through the use of world-spawned items and specific class perks like the **Axeman**, players can fight back or escape tight situations. Managing your inventory is just as important as managing your stamina.

## Killer Mechanics: The Rotten and Beyond

Playing as the killer in Bite by Night requires a mix of map pressure and mechanical skill. The most iconic killer, **The Rotten** (a copyright-friendly nod to Springtrap), utilizes a kit focused on area denial and ranged pressure.

### The Rotten's Ability Kit
1.  **Swing (M1):** The standard melee attack used to damage survivors.
2.  **Axe Throw (Q + M1):** A high-skill projectile that can injure survivors from a distance. If you lose your axe, you must retrieve it or down the survivor to reset the cooldown.
3.  **Bear Trap (E):** Deployable traps that root survivors in place. These are best placed in narrow corridors or directly in front of generators.
4.  **Scream/Howl:** A proximity-based ability that can disorient survivors or reveal their location.
5.  **Charge:** A burst of speed used to close the gap during a chase.

### The Doppelganger
Another formidable foe is the Doppelganger. This killer introduces unique visual distortions. When facing the Doppelganger, survivors may experience screen blurring or "hallucination" effects. 

> **Warning:** If your screen begins to shake or distort while facing the Doppelganger, you must manually shake your camera to clear the effect and regain visibility.

## Mastering the Mechanics: A Bite by Night Wiki Deep Dive

To succeed in higher-level lobbies, players must understand the nuances of stamina and environmental interaction. The "Forest" map, a staple of the current rotation, features long sightlines but plenty of foliage for survivors to hide in.

### Stamina Management
Both killers and survivors rely on stamina. For survivors, sprinting consumes stamina quickly. If you run out of breath while being chased, your movement speed drops significantly, making you an easy target for the killer's "Curb Stomp" finisher. 

### The Escape Phase
Once the clock hits 6:00 AM, the atmosphere of the game shifts. The exit gates become highlighted. This is the most dangerous part of the match, as killers will often "gate camp" or patrol the space between the final generators and the exits. Some maps feature multiple exits, while others force all survivors through a single chokepoint.

### Advanced Killer Strategies
*   **Trap Doubling:** Placing two bear traps in a "V" shape around a corner can catch even experienced survivors who try to jump over the first one.
*   **LMS (Last Man Standing):** When only one survivor remains, the killer gains a slight speed boost. Use this time to patrol the hatch or final exit gate aggressively.
*   **Axe Retrieval:** If you miss an axe throw, don't just leave it. The axe is your most powerful tool for stopping survivors who are about to reach a barricade.

## Map Knowledge and Navigation

The current 2026 map pool includes the Forest and various indoor facilities. Indoor maps favor the killer due to the tight corridors which make bear traps nearly impossible to avoid. Outdoor maps favor survivors who can use the "Bloxy Cola" to outrun killers across wide-open spaces.

| Map Feature | Survivor Advantage | Killer Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Forest Foliage** | Easy to hide and lose line-of-sight. | Harder for survivors to see traps in grass. |
| **Indoor Doorways** | Can be barricaded to stall the killer. | Perfect for "Scream" ability hits. |
| **Generator Clusters** | Fast completion if the killer is far. | Easy to patrol 3 generators at once. |

## FAQ

**Q: How do I escape the "The Rotten" when he is chasing me?**

A: The best strategy is to use a combination of barricades and the Taser. Try to lead the killer toward a door, barricade it, and then use the Taser if they attempt to break through or find an alternate route. Always save your Bloxy Cola for the final stretch to the exit.

**Q: What does the VIP Gamepass do in Bite by Night?**

A: The VIP Gamepass typically provides players with bonus points at the end of matches and access to exclusive cosmetic skins for both survivors and killers. It is a great way to support the developers while speeding up your progression toward unlocking perks like the Axeman.

**Q: How many generators need to be fixed according to the Bite by Night wiki?**

A: Survivors must repair exactly five generators to trigger the 6:00 AM escape phase. Each repaired generator provides a time bonus, which is crucial for ensuring the team has enough time to reach the exit gates once they open.

**Q: Can the killer destroy survivor items?**

A: While killers cannot directly destroy items like the Taser or Bloxy Cola, they can force survivors to use them prematurely through aggressive chasing. If a survivor is downed, any held items are dropped or lost depending on the specific server settings.
