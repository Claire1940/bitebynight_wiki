## The Roden: Starter Guide and Trap Placement

The Roden (often referred to as the Springtrap-style killer) is the most straightforward animatronic to pilot. Despite being the starter character, he remains incredibly effective due to his bleed mechanics and crowd control. To master **bite by night how to win as killer** with the Roden, you must utilize your bear traps effectively.

### Roden Ability Breakdown

| Ability | Input | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Swing** | M1 | A forward lunge and axe strike. Deals roughly 28 damage. |
| **Cleaver Throw**| Q | Throws the axe. Hits cause bleed damage and reveal the survivor's location. |
| **Bear Trap** | E | Places a trap. Survivors caught are immobilized for approximately 5 seconds. |
| **Scream** | Passive/E | Disorients nearby survivors, often usable after securing a kill. |

The most effective way to use the Roden is to place traps at "bottlenecks." These are areas survivors *must* pass through, such as the narrow hallways leading to generators or directly behind doors. Placing a trap behind a door makes it nearly invisible to a survivor running in a panic. If a survivor is "looping" you (running around an obstacle repeatedly), do not waste time chasing them indefinitely. Instead, drop a trap on one side of the loop or rotate to a different target.

## Mastering the Doppelganger: The Power of Deception

Many veteran players consider the Doppelganger to be the strongest killer currently available. His ability to blend in with the survivor team creates immense paranoia, slowing down generator repairs as players second-guess their teammates.

To succeed with the Doppelganger, use the **Skin Stealer** ability to transform into a human. This allows you to walk right up to survivors working on generators without triggering immediate panic. Once you are in range, revert and use your **Tentacle Arm** to snag them if they try to flee in a straight line.

### Doppelganger Tactics
1. **Wire Eyes:** Deploy your minions in high-traffic areas. They act as autonomous sentries that reveal survivors on your map.
2. **Skin Stealer Sabotage:** While disguised, you can feign working on a generator only to explode or sabotage it when other survivors join you.
3. **Targeting Low HP Players:** Once a player is revealed by your "squids" (minions), prioritize them. The Doppelganger excels at finishing off wounded prey.

## The Project: Advanced Stance Management

The Project is the most complex killer in Bite by Night because it requires constant switching between three modes. If you want to know **bite by night how to win as killer** at the highest levels of play, you must master these transitions.

| Mode | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Speed Mode** | Incredible mobility; includes a leap ability to close gaps. | Very low damage output. |
| **Strength Mode**| High damage per hit; great for finishing chases. | Slower movement speed. |
| **Stealth Mode** | Quiet movement; includes "Drill Stab" and "Burrow" abilities. | Requires precise timing to be effective. |

The optimal rotation for the Project is to stay in **Speed Mode** to patrol generators. Once a survivor is spotted, use the Leap to close the distance. After landing a hit, immediately switch to **Strength Mode** to maximize damage. If a survivor manages to break line of sight, switch to **Stealth Mode** and use the **Burrow (R)** ability to travel underground and surprise them from beneath.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Switching modes has a 5-second cooldown. If you switch to Speed Mode at the wrong time, you may find yourself unable to deal enough damage to down a survivor before they reach a safe zone.

## Core Strategies for Every Match

Regardless of which animatronic you choose, certain universal truths apply to the killer role. Following these rules will separate you from amateur players.

### 1. Don't Spam Your Attack
Every M1 swing has a roughly 2-second cooldown. If you swing and miss, the survivor gains significant distance. Only swing when you are 100% certain the hit will connect. This is especially important against "Fighters" who can parry your attacks if you are predictable.

### 2. Pressure the Generators
Survivors win by fixing generators and batteries to speed up the clock. Your primary job is to patrol these objectives. If you see a generator with high progress, damage it and linger in the area. Each generator fixed reduces the time until 6 AM, which works against you.

### 3. Handle "Loopers" and "Fighters"
*   **Loopers:** If a survivor is skilled at jumping through windows or running around fences, they are trying to waste your time. If you can't catch them in 15-20 seconds, leave them. Find a "weaker" survivor (like a Medic or Customer) who is easier to down.
*   **Fighters:** Watch for the axe animation. If a survivor is holding an axe, they will try to stun or parry you. Wait for them to swing first, then counter-attack during their recovery frames.

### 4. The 6 AM Exit Camp
When the clock hits 6 AM, the survivors will head toward the storage area exit. This is your final chance. Position yourself near the exit gates and place any remaining traps. Survivors are often desperate at this stage and will make mistakes, such as sprinting blindly into a bottleneck.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to earn scrap as a killer?**

A: You earn scrap by damaging survivors, downing them, and successfully eliminating them. Winning the match provides a massive bonus (up to 400 scrap), so focusing on the "bite by night how to win as killer" strategies is the most efficient way to grind currency for new animatronics.

**Q: Can survivors hide from me forever?**

A: No. While survivors can hide in lockers or dark corners, the game mechanics favor the killer as time passes. Abilities like the Roden's Scream or the Doppelganger's Wire Eyes are designed to reveal hiding players. Additionally, once it becomes "Last Man Standing," the final survivor's location is periodically revealed to you.

**Q: Why does my axe disappear when playing as the Roden?**

A: This happens if you use the "Throw Cleaver" (Q) ability. You must walk to the location where the axe landed to pick it up. Your damage is significantly reduced while you are unarmed, so always prioritize retrieving your weapon.

**Q: How do I deal with the taser?**

A: The taser used by Security Guards causes a blurry screen and a movement slow, but it does not completely stop you. Most experienced killers simply keep walking toward the survivor. The taser has a long cooldown, so once they use it, they are vulnerable.
