## Step-by-Step Strategy for Maximum Rewards

To hit the 525 cap and progress toward obtaining Agony, follow this specific gameplay loop in every public server. Note that **VIP servers do not grant rewards**, so you must play in public matches to see any progress.

### 1. Focus on Solo Generators
Completing a generator entirely by yourself yields significantly more rewards than "assisting" a teammate. While teamwork is essential for survival, if you want to maximize your payout, find a generator in a secluded area (like the Warehouse) and finish it from 0% to 100%. A single full completion can net you approximately **230 Scrap**.

### 2. Avoid High-Risk Tasks
The "Battery Task" is often a trap for newer players. Carrying a battery leaves you vulnerable, slows your movement, and makes you a primary target for the killer. Unless you are certain the killer is on the other side of the map, stick to generators and camera surveillance.

### 3. Use Cameras for "Wallhacks"
As a Security Guard, pressing 'E' to access cameras essentially gives you legal wallhacks. By highlighting the killer, you earn points for "Assistance" and "Discovery." This allows you to stay active in the game's economy even if you are hiding in a dark corner to ensure survival.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Agony in Bite by Night?**

A: The fastest method is consistently winning public matches as a Survivor. Focus on maximizing your performance score by completing solo generators and using your role's unique abilities (like the Security Guard's cameras) to assist the team.

**Q: Did the developers nerf the scrap farm recently?**

A: Yes, in early 2026, the maximum scrap per game was reduced from 1,000 to 525. However, the rate of earning has been increased, making it faster to reach the lower cap.

**Q: Can I farm Agony or Scrap in a private VIP server?**

A: No. Currency and progression are disabled in VIP servers to prevent exploit-based farming. You must play in public matchmaking to earn rewards.

**Q: Is the Security Guard really better than the Fighter for farming?**

A: For the average player, yes. The Security Guard allows you to earn points safely through cameras and provides a stun gun for defense. The Fighter has a much higher learning curve and carries a higher risk of dying early, which results in zero rewards.

**Q: How do I stop becoming the Killer every game?**

A: Navigate to the in-game settings menu and enable the "Disable Killer" option. This will ensure you are always assigned the Survivor role, which is better for consistent currency farming.
