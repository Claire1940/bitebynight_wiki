## Mastering the Mechanics: How to Use Technician BBN

The Technician’s power comes from three distinct passive and active abilities. To succeed, you must learn the timing for each, as misusing your stamina or mask duration can leave you vulnerable to the animatronics.

### 1. The Vanny Mask (Mask On)
Equipping the mask is the core of the Technician's gameplay. It isn't just a cosmetic choice; it serves as the catalyst for your most powerful movement options. When the mask is active, your vision changes, and you gain access to the "Phase" ability.

### 2. Mask On Phase (Wall Phasing)
This is arguably the most "broken" mechanic in Bite by Night. While the mask is active, you can phase through specific walls and obstacles. 

> **💡 Pro Tip:** You cannot phase through every wall in the game. Look for walls or crates that have subtle **pink pixelated outlines**. These indicate "glitched" geometry that the Technician can bypass.

To execute a phase, ensure your mask is equipped and double-click (or use the dedicated action button on mobile) while moving toward a compatible wall. This allows you to cut across the map or escape a killer who thinks they have you cornered in a room.

### 3. Static Mirage
The Static Mirage is your primary defensive tool. By pressing the **E key** (or the ability icon), you deploy a static field. If the killer enters this field, they are briefly stunned, typically for a duration of 5 seconds.

| Ability Component | Effect | Cooldown/Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **Stun Duration** | **5 Seconds** | Moderate Cooldown |
| **Killer Interaction** | Killer can break the field | Immediate if hit |
| **Deployment** | Places at current location | 1 active field at a time |

## Strategic Gameplay and Scrap Farming

To truly understand **how to use technician bbn**, you must adopt a "hit and run" playstyle. Since your base stats are slightly lower than the Fighter class, you should avoid prolonged chases in open areas. Instead, lure the killer toward pixelated walls or tight corridors where your Static Mirage is most effective.

### Escape Strategies
1. **The Double-Back Phase:** Lead the killer toward a phaseable crate. Double-click to go through it, then immediately deploy a Static Mirage on the other side. By the time the killer rounds the corner, they will hit the stun, giving you ample time to vanish.
2. **Generator Defense:** Place a Static Mirage near a high-priority generator. This forces the killer to either take the stun or spend time destroying the field, giving you a head start to flee.
3. **Stamina Management:** Phasing and running both consume stamina. Never use your mask to phase if your stamina bar is below 25%, as you will be unable to sprint away once you reach the other side of the wall.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I phase through walls as a Technician?**

A: First, ensure your Vanny mask is equipped. Approach a wall or object with pink pixelated effects and double-click your primary interaction button. If the wall is compatible, you will pass through it instantly. Note that this consumes a portion of your stamina.

**Q: Why is the Technician more expensive than the Fighter?**

A: While the Fighter has better base speed and stamina, the Technician's ability to bypass map geometry and stun killers with the Static Mirage provides higher utility. The 1,600 scrap cost reflects its status as a high-skill, high-reward class.

**Q: Can the killer destroy the Static Mirage?**

A: Yes, the killer can manually break the static field. However, doing so requires them to stop their pursuit and interact with the field, which still buys you valuable time to escape or finish a generator.

**Q: Is the Technician class bugged?**

A: Some players report issues with phasing not triggering. This is usually due to trying to phase through "normal" walls. Always look for the pink pixel indicators. If the pixels are present and it still fails, check your ping or ensure you have enough stamina to complete the action. Learning **how to use technician bbn** requires recognizing which parts of the environment are "glitched" and which are solid.
