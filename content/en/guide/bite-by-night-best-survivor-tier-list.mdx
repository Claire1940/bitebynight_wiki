## S-Tier: The Medic (Essential Support)

The Medic stands at the top of the **bite by night best survivor tier list** due to a mechanic that is often overlooked by casual players: Overhealth. While some argue that natural health regeneration makes healers redundant, the Medic’s ability to grant health *above* the maximum limit is a game-changer.

### Key Abilities
*   **Heal (Other):** Heals a teammate for 5 HP per second, up to a 50 HP limit. More importantly, it can provide up to 25 "Overhealth."
*   **Self-Heal:** Restores 20–40 HP to yourself. It cannot heal you past 74 HP, but it is far more consistent than the Customer's pizza.

The true power of the Medic lies in the fact that Overhealth can completely negate an entire attack. If Springtrap swings for 36 damage and you have even 1 point of Overhealth, that single point can sometimes absorb the impact of the entire hit, effectively giving your teammates an extra life.

## A-Tier: The Customer (The Reliable Runner)

The Customer is the starter survivor, but don't let that fool you. In the current 2026 meta, the Customer remains a top-tier pick because of their sheer "selfish" survivability. In a game where killers are punishingly fast, having a personal speed boost is often better than trying to help a teammate and dying in the process.

### Why Customer Ranks High
1.  **Drink Ability:** This provides a 5-second speed boost followed by a 15-second "Energetic" buff. This buff significantly reduces stamina drain, allowing you to outrun killers across large maps like the Forest.
2.  **Pizza:** While it only heals for 20% of your current HP (making it less effective at very low health), it provides enough sustain to keep you out of the "one-shot" range.
3.  **Accessibility:** As a free character, the Customer is the gold standard for learning map layouts and generator locations without the high-risk mechanics of the Fighter.

## A- Tier: The Fighter (High Risk, High Reward)

The Fighter is the most controversial entry on the **bite by night best survivor tier list**. In the hands of a professional with F1-driver reaction times, the Fighter can humiliate a killer. However, for the average player, the Fighter can feel like a liability.

### The Parry Problem
The Fighter relies on a **Parry** stance to stun the killer and gain charges for a heavy **Swing**. However, the window to react to a killer's M1 animation is incredibly tight—sometimes as low as 2/60ths of a second. If you miss a parry, you are inflicted with a **Weakness** debuff, making the killer's already massive damage even more lethal.

| Ability | Effect | Cooldown |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Swing** | 3-second stun; effects scale with charges. | 30 Seconds |
| **Parry** | 1.5-second stun on hit; adds 1 Swing charge. | Variable |

If you can master the timing, the Fighter is the only character capable of actively peeling the killer off a teammate. If you cannot, you are essentially playing a survivor with no defensive tools and average health.

## B-Tier: Security Guard (The Tactician)

The Security Guard offers a unique playstyle focused on information gathering. While they aren't the best at escaping a direct chase, they provide invaluable data that can help the entire team avoid the killer entirely.

*   **Security Cameras:** Allows you to view various map sectors and "ping" the killer, highlighting them for all survivors.
*   **Taser:** A projectile that slows the killer. The longer you charge it, the more significant the slow. 

The main drawback of the Security Guard is that charging the Taser forces you to walk. In most scenarios, walking while the killer is closing in is a death sentence. The Security Guard is best used as a "back-line" character who pings the killer while others work on generators. ## Pro Tips for All Survivors

Regardless of where your favorite character sits on the **bite by night best survivor tier list**, these universal strategies will increase your survival rate:

1.  **Stamina Management:** Never deplete your stamina bar fully unless you are in an active chase. Walking helps you stay quiet and ensures you have a full tank when the killer spots you.
2.  **Map Awareness:** In dark maps, killer hitboxes can feel larger than they appear. Always keep a mental map of the nearest "loop" or pallet.
3.  **Team Composition:** A perfect team usually consists of one Medic, one Security Guard (for pings), and two Runners (Customer) or one skilled Fighter.
4.  **Audio Cues:** Every killer has distinct footstep sounds and "proximity" cues. Learn these to know when to hide before the heartbeat starts.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing as the Medic, try to overheal your teammates *before* they enter a chase. The extra 25 HP is most effective when the survivor is at full health.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best survivor for solo players in Bite by Night?**

A: The **Customer** is widely considered the best for solo play. Their abilities are entirely self-sufficient, and the speed boost from the Drink ability allows you to escape bad situations without needing help from teammates.

**Q: Does the Medic's heal work while running?**

A: No. Using the Medic's heal or self-heal requires you to stand still. Attempting to move will cancel the ability, so always ensure you are in a safe, hidden location before attempting to patch up.

**Q: Is the Fighter worth the 1,000 currency cost?**

A: Only if you have excellent reaction times. The Fighter is currently the most difficult character to play in the **bite by night best survivor tier list**. If you struggle with parry timings in other games, you may find the Customer or Medic more rewarding.

**Q: How do I counter the Mimic's high damage?**

A: The Mimic deals 42 damage per hit, which can down most survivors in two to three swings. The best counter is the Medic's Overhealth or the Customer's stamina-reduction buff to stay out of reach entirely.
