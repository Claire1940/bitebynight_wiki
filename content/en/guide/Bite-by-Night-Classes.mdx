## The Customer: Reliable Survivalist

The Customer is the default choice among the **Bite by Night Classes**, provided to every player upon their first login. While it may lack the flashier stuns of the Fighter or the utility of the Security Guard, its self-sustain makes it a highly resilient pick for solo players.

### Customer Abilities
*   **Pizza (Q):** Heals the player for 20% of their missing health. With a 25-second cooldown, this allows you to recover from minor mistakes without needing a dedicated Medic.
*   **Drink (E):** Provides a temporary movement speed burst. This is your primary tool for breaking line of sight during a chase.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The "Drink" ability applies a slowness debuff immediately after the speed boost wears off. Only use it when you are certain you can reach a safe loop or a barricaded door before the crash hits.

## Fighter and Healer: The Support Core

For teams looking to coordinate, the Fighter and Healer classes provide the backbone of survival. The Fighter is designed to draw the killer's attention, while the Healer ensures that any mistakes made during those distractions aren't fatal.

### The Fighter Class
The Fighter is the only class that can consistently engage the killer head-on. By utilizing an axe, this class can apply significant stuns, though it requires precise timing.

*   **Swing (Q):** A lunge attack that strikes the killer, applying a 3.5 to 5-second stun. The effectiveness of this move scales based on your "Parry" charges.
*   **Parry (E):** A defensive stance that blocks incoming attacks. A successful parry stuns the killer for 1.5 seconds and grants a charge for your next Swing.

### The Healer Class
Often targeted first by experienced killers, the Healer (or Medic) is a selfless role. They lack offensive tools but are essential for long-drawn-out matches where health resources are scarce.

The Security Guard is currently considered the strongest of the **Bite by Night Classes** due to its unparalleled ability to track the killer and provide long-range support. At a price of 900 Scraps, it is the most expensive survivor role, but its kit justifies the investment.

### Why the Security Guard Excels
The Security Guard's strength lies in information. By using the **Cams (E)** ability, the player can cycle through map cameras to reveal the killer's position to the entire team. This prevents "sneaky" killers like the Mimic in stealth mode from getting easy jump scares.

Additionally, the **Taser (Q)** is a versatile defensive tool. Unlike the Fighter's axe, which requires close proximity, the Taser can be charged and fired from a distance. The longer the charge, the more severe the slow and stun applied to the animatronic. This makes the Security Guard the ultimate "staller," capable of keeping the killer at bay while teammates focus on generators.

## Objective Management and Survival Mechanics

Regardless of which of the **Bite by Night Classes** you choose, understanding the core mechanics is vital for victory. The primary goal is to survive until 6 AM, which can be accelerated by completing generators.

### Generator Steps
1.  **Color Matching:** Match the color blocks to their corresponding halves.
2.  **Light Toggle:** Ensure all lights in the secondary panel are switched on.
3.  **Manual Pull:** Move your cursor up and down to simulate pulling a lever.

Completing a generator significantly reduces the remaining time in the round. While doing these tasks, keep an eye on nearby doors. The **Barricade Mechanic** allows you to hold a door against a killer in a minigame where you must keep your mouse centered. Losing this minigame results in the door being destroyed and the survivor taking damage.

## Class Selection Strategy

When forming a lobby, a balanced team usually outperforms a group of four identical classes. A "Meta" team composition for 2026 typically includes:

1.  **One Security Guard:** For constant surveillance and marking generators.
2.  **One Fighter:** To peel the killer off vulnerable teammates.
3.  **One Healer:** To reset the team after a successful chase.
4.  **One Customer or Second Security Guard:** To focus purely on objectives and additional stalling.

## FAQ

**Q: Which of the Bite by Night Classes is best for solo players?**

A: The Customer is highly recommended for solo players. The "Pizza" ability provides self-healing that doesn't rely on teammates, and the "Drink" speed boost offers a reliable escape tool when you don't have a Fighter to protect you.

**Q: How do I unlock new classes in Bite by Night?**

A: Classes are unlocked in the in-game shop using Scraps. You earn Scraps by playing matches, with bonuses awarded for surviving until 6 AM, completing generators, and rescuing teammates.

**Q: Can the killer destroy the Security Guard's cameras?**

A: No, the cameras are static map elements. However, the killer can see when they are being "marked," alerting them that a Security Guard is currently watching their movements.

**Q: Does the Fighter's Parry work against all killer attacks?**

A: The Parry works against basic melee swings. However, it is less effective against area-of-effect abilities like the Mimic's Leap or Springtrap's Scream, which can blind or stun the Fighter before they can react.
