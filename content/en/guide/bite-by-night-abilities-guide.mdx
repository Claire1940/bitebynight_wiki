Before diving into specific kits, players must understand how to unlock new characters and abilities. The primary currency in the game is **Scraps**. You earn Scraps by completing matches, performing well in your role, and interacting with objectives. While some characters are available for free, the more complex animatronics require a significant investment.

## Animatronic Abilities and Move Sets

The animatronics are the stars of the show, each possessing a unique playstyle ranging from trap-setting to psychological warfare. In 2026, the meta has shifted toward high-mobility hunters who can track survivors across the map.

### Springtrap: The Relentless Hunter
Springtrap is the entry-level animatronic, but don't let the "free" price tag fool you. He is a formidable foe with a kit designed for tracking and area control. His primary weapon is an axe, which dictates his available moves.

*   **Axe Throw (Q):** Springtrap throws his axe. If it connects with a survivor, they are "Marked," allowing you to see their location through walls. You must manually pick the axe back up (F) to regain your full move set.
*   **Bear Trap (E - With Axe):** Places a physical trap on the ground. Survivors who step in this are immobilized for several seconds.
*   **Terrifying Scream (E - Without Axe):** If you have thrown your axe, your E ability changes to a scream that blinds nearby survivors, making it easier to close the distance for a manual takedown.

### Mimic: The Versatile Stalker
The Mimic is a 4,500 Scrap investment that offers three distinct "Modes." Switching between these modes is essential for high-level play.

| Mode | Primary Benefit | Unique Move |
|---|---|---|
| **Strength** | Increased M1 Damage | Heavy Strike |
| **Speed** | Faster Movement | Pounce (E) |
| **Stealth** | Silent Footsteps | Underground Ambush (R) |

The **Stealth Mode** is widely considered the strongest. While in this mode, you can use the **R ability** to travel fully underground, popping up behind unsuspecting survivors for a surprise grab.

### Ennard: The Master of Deception
At 6,500 Scraps, Ennard is the most expensive animatronic for a reason. His kit revolves around manipulation and physical displacement.

*   **Mechanical Lash (Q):** A mid-range attack that strikes survivors.
*   **The Grab (E):** Ennard reaches out and pulls a survivor directly toward him, negating their attempts to run away.
*   **Shapeshift (R):** This allows Ennard to take the form of other characters in the lobby. This is perfect for tricking survivors into thinking a friend is approaching when it is actually the killer.

## Survivor Abilities and Survival Tactics

Survivors rely on their chosen ability to escape the animatronics. Each ability has a specific cooldown and utility, and choosing the right one for your playstyle is vital for your **bite by night abilities guide** mastery.

### The Pizza Kit (Sustain & Speed)
The Pizza kit is the most common choice for beginners due to its simplicity.
*   **Eat (Q):** Consumes a slice to instantly regain 20 HP. This has a 30-second cooldown.
*   **Sugar Rush (E):** Provides a massive speed boost for a short duration. 
*   **The Downside:** Once the Sugar Rush ends, you suffer a "Slowness" debuff for a few seconds, making you vulnerable if you haven't reached safety.

### The Bat (Combat & Defense)
For players who prefer to fight back, the Bat offers stun mechanics.
*   **Swing (Q):** A melee strike that stuns the animatronic for a short period.
*   **Parry (E):** A high-skill move. If timed correctly against an animatronic's attack, it completely negates the damage and leaves the hunter dazed.

### The Security Guard (Information & Control)
The Security Guard is the "intel" role of the group.
*   **Cams (E):** Allows the player to check remote cameras. This also highlights the location of generators on the HUD for a limited time.
*   **Taser (Q):** A chargeable weapon. Holding Q increases the stun duration. At max charge, it can disable an animatronic for a significant amount of time.

### The Medic (Team Support)
The Medic is essential for long-term survival in high-rank matches.
*   **Passive:** Heals nearby allies for 5 HP every 2 seconds.
*   **Self-Heal (E):** Heals the user for 5 HP every 1 second. This has a 60-second cooldown and should only be used when in a secure location.

| Ability | Role | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Pizza** | Survivalist | Solo players and kiting |
| **Bat** | Protector | Defending teammates at objectives |
| **Security** | Scout | Finding generators and tracking the killer |
| **Medic** | Support | Keeping the team healthy during the endgame |

## Advanced Strategies for Success

To truly excel, you must combine these abilities with environmental awareness. For survivors, this means knowing which doors to close and when to use your limited resources. For animatronics, it means predicting survivor paths.

1.  **The "Scream-Charge" Combo:** As Springtrap, throw your axe to mark a survivor, then use your Scream (E) to blind them as you approach. While they are blinded, use a charged M1 attack for maximum damage.
2.  **The Taser Trap:** Security Guards should wait for an animatronic to begin a "Grab" animation before firing the Taser. This interrupts the animation and saves a teammate instantly.
3.  **Mimic Stealth Cycles:** When playing as Mimic, never stay in one mode for too long. Use Speed to traverse the map, then switch to Stealth once you are within 50 studs of a survivor to mask your audio cues.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always keep an eye on your battery life if you are using the Security Guard's cameras. Running out of battery in a chase will leave you without your Taser defense.

## FAQ

**Q: Which animatronic is the best for beginners in Bite by Night?**

A: Springtrap is the best choice for beginners. Not only is he free to unlock, but his axe-marking mechanic helps new players learn the map layouts and survivor movement patterns without needing complex resource management.

**Q: Can survivors heal each other without the Medic ability?**

A: No, standard survivors cannot heal each other unless they are using the Medic ability or have found specific world items. The Pizza kit only allows for self-healing, making the Medic a vital part of any coordinated team.

**Q: How do I earn Scraps faster to unlock the **bite by night abilities guide** characters?**

A: The fastest way to earn Scraps is by completing objectives (generators) as a survivor or achieving "Takedowns" as an animatronic. Winning the match provides a significant Scrap bonus, as does playing on higher-difficulty maps.

**Q: Does the Mimic's underground move make him invincible?**

A: While underground, the Mimic cannot be hit by standard survivor attacks like the Bat swing. However, he can still be detected by the Security Guard's camera highlights, so stealthy movement is still required.
