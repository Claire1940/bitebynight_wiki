## The Warehouse: Industrial Terror

While The Forest provides an open-air experience, the Warehouse map focuses on claustrophobia and industrial hazards. This location is characterized by high ceilings, stacks of shipping crates, and narrow hallways that make it difficult to spot a killer like Springtrap until they are right on top of you.

The Warehouse utilizes verticality more than the Forest. Players can often find catwalks or elevated office spaces that provide a bird's-eye view of the main floor. However, being caught on a catwalk can be a death sentence if the killer has high mobility or ranged abilities.

### Strategic Elements of the Warehouse

1.  **Shipping Containers:** These provide excellent hiding spots but can also lead to "dead zones" where a survivor can easily be cornered.
2.  **Industrial Machinery:** Some machines can be interacted with to create noise distractions, helping to lead the killer away from a nearly completed objective.
3.  **Loading Docks:** These large doors often serve as the final escape gates once all objectives are powered.

## Strategic Tips for Map Navigation

To excel in *Bite by Night*, you must treat the map as a tool. Here are several expert strategies for navigating these lethal environments:

*   **Learn the "Jungle Gyms":** Most maps contain specific clusters of walls, windows, and pallets known as jungle gyms. Memorizing the layout of these structures allows you to "loop" the killer for extended periods, giving your teammates time to complete objectives.
*   **Identify the "Killer Shack":** A staple of the asymmetrical genre, most maps have a high-priority building (like the central cabin in the Forest) that contains a basement and a very strong window-pallet combo. Use this resource sparingly.
*   **Watch the Perimeter:** Exit gates are always located on the outer walls of the map. Once the final objective is complete, immediately head toward the nearest gate, but stay in the shadows to avoid being intercepted.
*   **Utilize Class Passives:** Some classes have abilities that reveal the aura of objectives or the killer within a certain range. Use these to plan your pathing through the **Bite by Night map** without running into a dead end.

> **Tip:** Always look for the "Red Stain" (the glow in front of the killer). This indicates which way they are facing, allowing you to hide behind environmental objects effectively even in open maps like The Forest.

## FAQ

**Q: How many maps will be available at the release of Bite by Night?**

A: Currently, The Forest and the Warehouse are the primary maps confirmed for the initial release phase. However, the developers have indicated that more locations are under development and will be added in post-launch updates.

**Q: Are the Bite by Night map layouts procedurally generated?**

A: While the core landmarks and boundaries of each **Bite by Night map** are fixed, the placement of smaller objects like pallets, windows, and objectives (generators) follows a semi-randomized pattern. This ensures that every match feels unique while allowing players to learn the general geography of the area.

**Q: Can killers interact with the map environment?**

A: Yes. Killers can break dropped pallets, damage generators to regress progress, and in some cases, interact with specific map hazards to trap or slow down survivors. Some killers may also have map-wide abilities that change the environment's lighting or soundscape.

**Q: What is the best map for beginners?**

A: The Forest is generally considered more beginner-friendly due to its open spaces, which make it easier to keep track of the killer's location. The Warehouse requires more advanced knowledge of "looping" and map geometry due to its tight corners and line-of-sight blockers.
