## Essential Byte by Night Settings for Pro Players

Before you even step foot into a match, your first priority should be optimizing your client. High-level play in **byte by night** requires precise movement and awareness, which can be hindered by default visual effects and lag. Adjusting your settings can provide a significant competitive advantage, especially when trying to spot a killer lurking in the distance.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Camera Shake** | **OFF** | Eliminates distractions during chases and helps maintain focus on the killer's position. |
| **Show Performance** | **ON** | Allows you to monitor ping and server age to avoid lag-induced deaths. |
| **Potato Mode** | **ON (Low-end PCs)** | Drastically increases FPS on mobile or older hardware, ensuring smoother skill checks. |
| **Hitboxes** | **ON** | Outlines players in red, making it significantly easier for killers to land attacks. |
| **Show Barriers** | **ON** | Highlights invisible walls, preventing you from getting stuck while running for your life. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your ping in the top-left corner. If your ping exceeds 100ms, consider switching servers, as high latency can cause killers to hit you through walls or doors.

## Survivor Roles and Abilities

Survival in **byte by night** is not just about hiding; it is about utilizing specific class abilities to stall the killer and complete objectives. Each role has a distinct playstyle, and a balanced team is often the most successful.

### The Security Guard
The Security Guard is widely considered the most powerful information-gathering role in 2026. By using the tablet, you can access cameras placed around the map to "mark" the killer. This highlight is visible to the entire team, allowing everyone to stay on the opposite side of the map.

### The Fighter
For players who prefer a more aggressive approach, the Fighter offers the ability to directly stun the killer. However, this role has a steep learning curve. You must master the **Parry** mechanic to gain charges for your axe swing. A successful parry stuns the animatronic for 2 seconds and prepares your next offensive move.

### The Medic
The Medic is essential for long-term survival. Matches often stretch into the early morning hours, and health becomes a rare resource. The Medic can heal teammates, which is vital since the "Pizza" item only restores a small amount of HP per use.

| Role | Primary Ability | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Security Guard** | Camera Monitoring & Marking | Information and Team Support |
| **Fighter** | Axe Swing & Parry | Defensive Aggression and Stalling |
| **Medic** | Healing Teammates | Sustained Survival and Recovery |
| **Fodder/Basic** | Item Consumption | Objective Focus and Distraction |

## Mastering the Animatronics: Killer Strategies

Playing as the killer in **byte by night** requires a different mindset. Your goal is to intercept survivors before they can power the exit gates or reach 6:00 AM. In the current 2026 update, the **Mimic** stands out as the most versatile and dangerous animatronic available.

### The Mimic's Modes
The Mimic can swap between three distinct modes, making it nearly impossible to predict:
1. **Strength Mode:** Focuses on raw damage and standard M1 attacks.
2. **Speed Mode:** Increases movement speed and allows for a pounce attack that ragdolls survivors.
3. **Stealth Mode:** The most "broken" ability in the game. The Mimic can drill into the ground, gaining thermal vision of every survivor on the map. Emerging from the ground deals Area of Effect (AoE) damage and stuns nearby players.

### Enard and Springtrap
While the Mimic is a top-tier choice, Enard offers unique psychological horror by disguising himself as other survivors. This can lead to devastating betrayals if the survivor team isn't communicating via external apps like Discord. Springtrap remains a solid choice for beginners, offering a straightforward kit focused on high-pressure chases.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not "cross-team" with the killer. Developers have implemented strict banning policies for players caught coordinating between teams in public servers.

## Map Overview and Navigation

There are currently three primary maps in **byte by night**, each requiring a different tactical approach.

1. **The Forest:** The largest and most open map. It is notorious for being laggy on mobile devices. Survivors should use the dense trees to break line-of-sight, while killers should utilize mobility skills to cover the vast distances between generators.
2. **The Warehouse:** A balanced map with a mix of indoor and outdoor sections. It features several "safe" loops and office spaces where survivors can barricade doors to buy time.
3. **The Pizzeria:** A classic horror setting with tight corridors and many dead ends. This map favors killers who can trap survivors in corners, such as Bonnie or the Mimic.

| Map | Size | Difficulty (Survivor) | Key Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Forest** | Large | Medium | Use trees for stealth; watch for Stealth Drill. |
| **Warehouse** | Medium | Easy | Master the barricade mechanics in offices. |
| **Pizzeria** | Small | Hard | Avoid dead ends; keep doors open for escape. |

## How to Farm Scrap Fast

Scrap is the primary currency in **byte by night**, used to unlock new animatronics, survivors, and cosmetics. To maximize your earnings in 2026, follow these efficiency tips:

*   **Play as the Killer:** On average, killers earn between 500-550 scrap per match in public servers, which is significantly higher than the average survivor payout.
*   **Complete Objectives:** As a survivor, you earn scrap for every generator you fix and every battery you plug in. Don't just hide; actively participate in the tasks.
*   **The VIP Pass:** If you are serious about the game, the VIP Gamepass is a must-buy. It provides a permanent **20% Scrap boost** and a **50% XP boost**. You can purchase this for 300 Robux or 15,000 Scrap.
*   **Security Guard Marking:** Using the Security Guard's ability to mark the killer provides bonus scrap for "Information Support."

## Winning the Endgame: 4:00 AM to 6:00 AM

Once the generators are powered and the batteries are set, the game enters its final phase. The objective often shifts to "Survive until 6:00 AM." During this time, the killer becomes more desperate, and their abilities may have reduced cooldowns.

**Survivor Strategy:**
*   **Don't Group Up:** One stealth attack from the Mimic can wipe out an entire team if they are standing together.
*   **Conserve Items:** Save your Soda (stamina boost) and Pizza (health) for the final two-minute sprint.
*   **Barricade Wisely:** Use the door barricade mechanic only when you know the killer is right behind you. Holding the door too early gives the killer time to find a different entrance.

**Killer Strategy:**
*   **Patrol the Gates:** Survivors will naturally gravitate toward the exit areas. 
*   **Use the Last Man Reveal:** When only one survivor remains, the game often highlights their general location. Use this to end the match quickly.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to escape the killer in Byte by Night?**

A: The most effective way is to break line-of-sight using the environment and then crouch to hide your footsteps. If you are playing as the Fighter, a well-timed parry can stun the killer, giving you a 2-second window to reach a safe zone or barricaded room.

**Q: How do I unlock the Mimic or Enard?**

A: These high-tier animatronics can be purchased in the in-game shop using Scrap. The Mimic costs 4,500 Scrap, which can be earned through regular play or boosted significantly if you have the VIP Gamepass.

**Q: Why does the timer sometimes go backward?**

A: In **byte by night**, certain game events or survivor deaths can cause the time to regress. This is a mechanic designed to punish the survivor team for losing members and to give the killer more time to secure a full victory.

**Q: Is the Fighter role better than the Security Guard?**

A: It depends on your skill level. The Security Guard is easier to use and provides better team-wide benefits through marking. The Fighter is technically stronger in a 1v1 scenario, but requires excellent ping and precise timing to be effective. For most players in 2026, the Security Guard is the more reliable choice for winning matches.
