## Performance Optimization for Bite by Night Potato Mode

To achieve the best results in **Bite by Night Potato Mode**, players should focus on reducing the load on the GPU and CPU by adjusting specific in-game and system-level settings. High-resolution textures and complex lighting are the primary culprits for frame drops in the Warehouse and Pizzeria maps.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact on Performance |
|---|---|---|
| **Graphics Quality** | Low / 1-3 | **High** - Reduces draw distance and shadow complexity. |
| **Shadows** | Off | **Medium** - Disabling real-time shadows significantly boosts FPS. |
| **Global Illumination** | Disabled | **High** - Essential for maintaining frames in dark areas. |
| **Anti-Aliasing** | Off | **Low** - Sharpens edges but consumes GPU resources. |
| **Texture Resolution** | Low | **Medium** - Reduces VRAM usage on older cards. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always close background applications like web browsers or recording software before launching the game to free up system memory for the AI processing of killers like the Mimic.

## Hidden Lore and Environmental Secrets

The Warehouse map serves as a manufacturing facility for Fazbear Robotics. Exploring this area reveals the darker side of the game's lore through environmental storytelling and hidden messages.

1.  **Robotics Division Room:** Contains inactive endo suits that resemble the Mimic Mark 2, complete with neural network links.
2.  **The Restricted Box:** A cracked shipping container in the warehouse displays the message: *"Did you think I'd escape?"* This box was originally intended for the Fazbear Robotics Division and noted a "behavioral anomaly."
3.  **The Status Board:** A hidden room requires a battery to power a panel. Once active, it displays the status of several animatronics:
    *   **Fredbear:** Inactive (Occasionally hostile)
    *   **Toy Freddy/Chica:** Dismantled
    *   **Spring Bonnie:** Breached
    *   **Marionette:** Missing

> **⚠️ Warning:** The "Don't listen to the girl's voice" signs in the forest refer to the Mimic's ability to replicate voices, including those of family members, to lure survivors into a trap.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Bite by Night Potato Mode remove the secret messages?**

A: No, while textures may be blurrier, the text on the "It's Me" door and the warehouse boxes remains visible. You may need to stand closer to the objects to trigger the higher-detail mipmaps for reading.

**Q: Can I still play the arcade games on low settings?**

A: Yes, the arcade machines in the lobby remain fully playable. Even with a **Bite by Night Potato Mode** configuration, the Snake mini-game is lightweight and should run smoothly.

**Q: Why does the Mimic do so little damage in Speed mode?**

A: Speed mode is balanced to prevent the killer from being unstoppable. The 75% damage reduction ensures that the killer must use the speed to position themselves rather than simply spamming attacks while moving at high velocity.

**Q: How do I access the secret status room in the Warehouse?**

A: You must find a battery and insert it into the wall panel near the endoskeleton assembly line. This will light up the wall showing the status of Fredbear, Spring Bonnie, and the others.
