## Essential Movement and Interaction Controls

Before diving into specific roles, every player must understand the foundational movement system. The game utilizes a standard WASD configuration, but the nuances of stamina management and environmental interaction are where many beginners struggle. Sprinting is not infinite; it consumes a stamina bar that requires careful management to avoid being left exhausted during a pursuit.

| Action | Keybinding | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Move Forward/Back/Left/Right** | W / A / S / D | Standard directional movement. |
| **Sprint** | Left Shift | Consumes stamina; use in short bursts. |
| **Crouch** | C | Reduces noise and helps in hiding behind cover. |
| **Interact** | E | Used for generators, picking up items, and objectives. |
| **Flashlight** | F | Toggles light (Survivors only). Use sparingly to avoid detection. |
| **Barricade Door** | F / E | Context-sensitive when standing near door frames. |

> **💡 Tip:** Avoid spamming the Shift key. If your stamina hits zero while being chased, your movement speed drops significantly, making you an easy target for any nearby animatronic.

## Survivor Role Controls and Abilities

In Bite by Night, your role determines your secondary and tertiary abilities, mapped to the **E** and **Q** keys. Understanding these specific **bite by night controls** is vital for team synergy. For instance, a Medic who forgets their Q-bind cannot heal teammates, leading to a quick team wipe.

### Survivor Role Ability Breakdown

| Role | E Ability | Q Ability |
|---|---|---|
| **Customer** | Pizza (Heal 20% HP) | Drink (Stamina/Speed Buff) |
| **Medic** | Self-Heal (Full HP) | Heal Teammate (Full HP) |
| **Fighter** | Axe Swing (Stuns Killer) | Parry (Deflects Attacks) |
| **Security Guard** | Camera View (Map Intel) | Taser (Disables Killer) |

The **Customer** role is the default starting point for most players. While the pizza provides a quick health boost, the drink is arguably more powerful for escaping tight situations, though it does come with a cooldown that slows stamina regeneration temporarily after the buff wears off.

The **Security Guard** requires a higher skill ceiling. Using the cameras (E) allows you to highlight the killer's position for the entire team, but it leaves you stationary and vulnerable. Mastering the taser (Q) requires precise timing, as missing the shot often results in an immediate down.

## Killer and Animatronic Controls

Playing as the Killer is mechanically simpler in terms of movement, but it demands better accuracy and cooldown management. The most popular killer in 2026, The Rotten (Springtrap), relies heavily on projectile aiming and trap placement.

### Animatronic Ability Mechanics

*   **M1 (Left Click):** Standard Attack/Lunge. This usually deals around 28 damage but has a strict 2-second cooldown. You cannot "spam" clicks; you must time your swings to coincide with the survivor's movement.
*   **Q Ability:** Projectile Attack. For characters like The Rotten, this involves throwing an axe. If it hits, it applies a bleed effect. If you miss, you must physically walk to the axe to pick it back up.
*   **E Ability:** Utility/Trap. This allows you to place bear traps at choke points. Traps snare survivors for approximately 5 seconds, providing a window for an easy kill.

| Killer Action | Keybinding | Strategic Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Attack** | Left Click | Use during a lunge to close small gaps. |
| **Throw Weapon** | Q | Use on survivors running in a straight line. |
| **Place Trap** | E | Set these near generators or narrow doorways. |
| **Interact/Break** | E | Destroy barricades or interact with lockers. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** When you throw your weapon (Q), your primary attack (M1) deals significantly less damage until you retrieve the weapon. Only throw if you are confident in your aim.

## Optimized Settings for Competitive Play

To truly master the **bite by night controls**, you must ensure your game settings are not hindering your performance. Roblox horror games are notorious for camera shake and lighting effects that can cause motion sickness or obscure the killer.

1.  **Shift Lock:** Enable this in the Roblox escape menu. It is essential for Killers to aim their projectiles and for Survivors to navigate tight corners while looking backward.
2.  **Camera Shake:** Toggle this **OFF** in the in-game settings. It provides a more stable view during chases, allowing for better spatial awareness.
3.  **Performance Mode:** Turn this **ON** to see your ping and server age. In a game where millisecond-perfect parries matter, knowing your latency is crucial.
4.  **Potato Mode:** If you experience frame drops, this mode removes unnecessary textures. Higher FPS directly correlates to more responsive input for your **bite by night controls**.

## Advanced Objective Controls

The game isn't just about running; it's about the objectives. Survivors must fix five generators and submit batteries to power the exit gates.

### Generator and Barricade Interaction

When interacting with a generator (E), you may encounter skill checks or progress bars. Staying stationary is dangerous, so listen for the killer's proximity heartbeat. 

Barricading is another vital mechanic. By pressing **F** or **E** near a door, you can set up a barrier. A circular UI element will appear; you must keep the cursor within the designated zone to successfully hold the door against a killer's assault. This "struggle" mechanic is a key part of high-level defensive play in 2026.

| Objective | Control | Importance |

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I sprint even though I'm holding Shift?**

A: Check your stamina bar at the bottom of the screen. If it is empty, you cannot sprint until it regenerates. Also, ensure you haven't recently used a "Drink" buff that is currently in its cooldown phase.

**Q: How do I change my bite by night controls or keybindings?**

A: Currently, the game does not support full custom keybinding within the in-game menu. You must use the default WASD and E/Q/F/C layout. Most players find these standard enough for comfortable play.

**Q: What does the "Shift Lock" setting do for the Killer?**

A: Shift Lock centers your mouse and locks the camera to your character's back. This is vital for Killers like The Rotten when aiming the axe throw (Q), as it allows for "crosshair" style aiming rather than relying on the free-moving cursor.

**Q: Can I use a controller to play Bite by Night?**

A: While Roblox supports controllers, the specific **bite by night controls** for abilities (E/Q) may not be perfectly mapped on all gamepads. It is highly recommended to use a keyboard and mouse for the precision required for stuns and projectile hits.
