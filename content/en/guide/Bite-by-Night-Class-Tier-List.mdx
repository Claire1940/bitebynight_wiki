## S-Tier: The Security Guard

The Security Guard currently sits at the top of our **Bite by Night Class Tier List** due to an incredibly versatile kit that provides both offensive disruption and vital information. Unlike other roles that focus on direct survival, the Security Guard acts as a "pest" that can completely derail a killer's momentum from a safe distance.

### Key Abilities
*   **Taser:** A chargeable projectile that, when landed, slows the killer to a crawl. This is particularly effective because it can be used while the killer is mid-animation, and the effect persists once the animation ends.
*   **Security Camera:** Allows the user to cycle through map cameras to mark the killer and locate generators. Marking the killer reveals their aura to the entire team, providing a massive tactical advantage.

### Strategic Tech: The Anti-Loop Counter
One of the reasons the Security Guard is so highly rated in 2026 is their ability to counter specific killer mechanics. For instance, when facing killers like Springtrap, who can use a scream ability to blind survivors, a skilled Security Guard can use the camera scan to reveal the killer's location during the blind. This effectively renders the killer's anti-loop utility useless and allows the team to continue their pathing without interruption.

## A-Tier: The Fighter and The Medic

These roles are the backbone of any successful survivor team. While they may not have the map-wide presence of a Security Guard, their contributions during chases and post-chase recovery are invaluable.

### The Fighter: The True Stunner
The Fighter is the only role in the game capable of completely immobilizing the killer. This makes them the primary protector of the group.

*   **Parry:** A defensive move that stuns the killer for a short duration if timed correctly.
*   **Lunge:** A follow-up attack that can extend the stun duration.
*   **Passive:** Fighters start the match with one free stun stack, meaning they don't have to land a parry immediately to be useful.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If a killer baits out your parry and you miss the timing, you are left completely vulnerable. Use your parry only when the killer is forced to commit to an attack.

### The Medic: The Sustainability Expert
The Medic is essential for long-term survival. Their ability to "Overheal" teammates provides a buffer of extra health (similar to a dodge meter) that can save a survivor from a lethal blow.

| Ability | HP Restored | Special Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Player Heal** | ~50 HP | Can provide **Overheal** for extra protection. |
| **Self-Heal** | ~20 HP | Keeps the Medic in the fight without needing help. |

In the 2026 meta, Medics are often the first target for killers. If you play this role, stay out of sight and only move in to heal once the killer has been distracted or stunned by a Fighter.

## B-Tier: The Customer

The Customer is the starter role and functions primarily as a solo survivalist. While they are ranked lower on the **Bite by Night Class Tier List**, they are the undisputed kings of the "Last Man Standing" (LMS) scenario.

### Stalling Capabilities
The Customer’s kit is built around keeping themselves alive and away from the killer. 
1.  **Bloxy Cola:** Provides a 5-second speed boost and 15 seconds of reduced stamina drain. This allows for massive distance gain during a chase.
2.  **Pizza:** A percentage-based heal (roughly 20% of current HP). This is best used when your health is low to maximize the efficiency of the heal.

The main drawback of the Customer is their lack of team utility. They cannot heal others, stun the killer, or provide intel. They are effectively "dead weight" for the team's objectives until they are the last ones left, where their stalling power finally shines.

## How to Choose Your Class

Selecting a role from the **Bite by Night Class Tier List** should depend on your preferred playstyle:

*   **If you like being a nuisance:** Choose the **Security Guard**. Focus on landing tasers from a distance and keeping the killer marked on cameras.
*   **If you enjoy high-risk, high-reward plays:** The **Fighter** is for you. Mastering the parry timing can single-handedly win games.
*   **If you are a support player:** The **Medic** is your best bet. Focus on communication to find injured teammates quickly.
*   **If you prefer playing solo:** Stick with the **Customer**. Use your Bloxy Cola to outrun the killer and survive until the hatch or exit gates are the only options left.

## FAQ

**Q: Which role is best for solo queuing in the Bite by Night Class Tier List?**

A: The Customer is generally best for solo players because they don't rely on teammates for healing or protection. However, if you have good mechanical skill, the Security Guard offers more carry potential.

**Q: Can the Medic overheal themselves?**

A: No, the Overheal mechanic (which provides extra health/dodge meter) is typically reserved for the "Player Heal" ability used on teammates. The Self-Heal only restores a flat amount of HP.

**Q: Does the Fighter's stun work on all killers?**

A: Yes, the Fighter's parry and lunge will stun any killer currently in the game, though some killers may have shorter stun recovery times than others.

**Q: How do I reveal generators as a Security Guard?**

A: While using the security cameras, you can scan the environment. If a generator is within the camera's field of view, it will be highlighted for a duration for all survivors on the map.
