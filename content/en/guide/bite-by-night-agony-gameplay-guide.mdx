## Essential Settings for Competitive Play

Before you even join a match, your first step should be optimizing your client. In a game where a single frame can determine if a taser hits or a killer's axe connects, performance is everything. Many players overlook the settings menu, but a few quick adjustments can provide a significant competitive edge.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Camera Shake** | **OFF** | Eliminates visual clutter and helps maintain focus during high-intensity chases. |
| **Performance Mode** | **ON** | Displays your ping and server age, which is vital for timing parries and dodges. |
| **Potato Mode** | **Optional** | Essential for mobile players or those on low-end PCs to maintain a stable 60 FPS. |
| **Shift Lock** | **ON** | Highly recommended for Killers (especially Rotten) to improve aiming with projectiles. |
| **Show Barriers** | **ON** | Helps beginners understand the physical limits of the map and pathfinding. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always play with your sound on and use high-quality headphones. The directional audio in Bite by Night is incredibly precise, allowing you to hear a killer's footsteps or a generator's hum through walls.

## Playing as the Animatronic: The Hunter's Toolkit

As a killer, your primary objective is to prevent the survivors from reaching 6:00 AM. You do this by patrolling generators, setting traps, and using your unique abilities to down players. In the current 2026 meta, the "Rotten" (Springtrap) remains a top-tier choice for beginners and veterans alike due to his versatile kit.

### The Rotten (Springtrap) Abilities
The Rotten excels at area denial and sustained pressure. His axe is a lethal tool that can be used both in close quarters and at range.

| Ability | Input | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Lunge Swing** | **M1 (Left Click)** | A forward dash followed by an axe swing. Deals approximately 28 damage. |
| **Axe Throw** | **Q** | Throws the axe as a projectile. Hits cause **Bleed Damage**, slowly draining survivor HP. |
| **Bear Trap** | **E** | Places a hidden trap. Snails survivors for 5 seconds when triggered. |

When playing as the Rotten, do not simply spam your M1. There is a 2-second cooldown on swings, and a missed attack gives survivors a massive window to escape or stun you. Instead, use your Bear Traps at "choke points"—narrow hallways or doorways that survivors must pass through to reach generators or the exit.

## Survivor Classes: Choosing Your Role

Survivors in Bite by Night are not defenseless, but their strength lies in their class abilities. You earn "Scraps" by playing the game, which can be spent in the shop to unlock more specialized roles. For those following this **bite by night agony gameplay guide**, understanding which class fits your playstyle is the first step toward consistent wins.

### Comparison of Survivor Roles

| Class | Cost | Primary Utility | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Customer** | **Free** | Self-healing (Pizza) and Stamina buffs (Drink). | Beginners learning the map. |
| **Medic** | **Scrap** | Heals self and teammates to full HP. | Team players and group survival. |
| **Fighter** | **Scrap** | Can stun the animatronic with an axe and parry attacks. | Aggressive players who like to loop. |
| **Security Guard** | **Scrap** | Access to security cameras and a long-range Taser. | Tactical players and shot-callers. |

### Advanced Survivor Tactics
Survival is a game of resource management. Your stamina is your most precious asset. Avoid sprinting unnecessarily; if you are caught with an empty stamina bar, you are a sitting duck. 

1. **Barricading:** If you are being chased, look for doors that can be barricaded. This mini-game requires you to keep a circle centered on your screen. A successful barricade can buy your team 10-15 seconds of precious time.
2. **Generator Priority:** Focus on generators that are in "dead zones" (areas with few escape routes) early in the game while the killer is distracted. Save the "safe" generators for the final hours.
3. **Healing Etiquette:** If you are playing as a Medic, prioritize healing the Fighter or Security Guard. They are the ones who will protect you when the killer closes in.

## The Economy: Farming Scraps Efficiently

Scraps are the lifeblood of progression in Bite by Night. Without them, you cannot unlock the more powerful animatronics or survivor classes. While you earn scraps naturally by playing, there are ways to maximize your income per match.

*   **As a Killer:** Every kill grants a scrap bonus. However, the real money comes from preventing generator completion. A "Clean Sweep" (killing everyone before 6 AM) provides the highest payout, often exceeding 400 scraps.
*   **As a Survivor:** Fixing generators is your primary source of income. You receive scraps for every percentage of progress you contribute. Even if you die, the scraps you earned from objectives are kept, making "suicide runs" on generators a viable (though risky) strategy for farming.

> **Warning:** Do not stay AFK unless you have AFK mode toggled on in the settings. Being inactive in a match reduces your scrap gain and makes you an easy target for killers looking for a quick win.

## The Final Hour: Surviving the 6:00 AM Rush

When the clock hits 6:00 AM, the game enters its most dangerous phase. The exit gates open, and the survivors must make a mad dash for freedom. As a killer, this is your last chance to secure victory.

**Killer Strategy (The Gate Camp):**
Memorize the exit locations for each map. In many maps, like the Storage Warehouse, the exit is tucked away in a corner. As the clock nears 6:00 AM, move toward the exit and set your remaining Bear Traps. Survivors are often frantic and less likely to look at the floor as they sprint for the finish line.

**Survivor Strategy (The Bait and Switch):**
If you are healthy, consider being the "bait." Draw the killer away from the exit so your injured teammates can escape. If you have a Taser or a Stun ability, save it specifically for the exit gate. A well-timed stun at 5:59 AM can be the difference between a team escape and a total wipe.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best killer for beginners in the bite by night agony gameplay guide?**

A: The Rotten (Springtrap) is widely considered the best starter killer. His abilities are straightforward, and his Bear Traps allow for passive map control even when you aren't actively chasing someone.

**Q: How do I counter the Doppelganger animatronic?**

A: The Doppelganger relies on tricking players by mimicking dead survivors. The best counter is to keep a mental note of who has died. If you see a "dead" player walking toward you, it is almost certainly the Doppelganger.

**Q: Does the "Drink" buff from the Customer class have a downside?**

A: Yes. While the Drink gives you a temporary stamina boost allowing you to run for twice as long, once it wears off, your stamina regeneration is significantly slowed for a short period. Use it only as a last resort during a chase.

**Q: Can I play Bite by Night on mobile?**

A: Yes, the game is fully compatible with mobile. However, make sure to turn on "Potato Mode" in the settings to ensure the game runs smoothly during intense encounters.
