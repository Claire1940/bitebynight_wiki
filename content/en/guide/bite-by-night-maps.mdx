### The Pizzeria: Navigating the Classic Layout

The Pizzeria is the flagship map of the game, heavily inspired by the original Five Nights at Freddy's but significantly expanded for a modern multiplayer experience. This map is characterized by its central stage area and the two long, high-tension hallways that lead directly to the security office. 

Unlike older iterations of this layout, the **bite by night maps** version removes "Pirates Cove," replacing it with a new entrance room near the main stage. This change facilitates better player flow and prevents "camping" in specific corners. The backstage area has also seen a massive overhaul, now featuring a break room and a loading bay, providing survivors with multiple flanking routes if they find themselves pursued near the stage.

#### Key Rooms in the Pizzeria

*   **The Security Office:** The heart of the map. It features two doors and two light buttons. Closing doors consumes power rapidly, so they should only be used when an animatronic is physically at the threshold.
*   **The Kitchen:** This is now a standalone room rather than a hallway attachment. It provides a decent hiding spot but can become a death trap if the hunter knows you are inside.
*   **The Play Structure:** A large area featuring a ball pit and climbing frames. This area offers verticality, allowing survivors to lose hunters by navigating through the complex tubes.
*   **The Arcade:** Located near the right hallway, this room is filled with cabinets that provide visual cover but offer limited exit points.

> **💡 Tip:** In the Pizzeria, never let your power drop below 15% before the final minute. If the power cuts out, you lose all defensive capabilities in the office, making you an easy target.

### The Forest: Survival in the Open

The Forest map represents a radical departure from the traditional indoor gameplay of the Pizzeria. This map is vast, featuring multiple paths, tents, and environmental barriers that can be used to break line-of-sight. The core challenge here is not managing doors, but rather maintaining spatial awareness.

As the match progresses, a unique environmental mechanic triggers: the map slowly takes on a deep red hue. This visual shift significantly reduces visibility and increases the psychological pressure on survivors. One of the most critical landmarks is the **Afton Residence**, a structure that often becomes the focal point for late-game confrontations.

| Feature | Description | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tents** | Small hiding spots scattered around. | Use for quick concealment, but avoid staying too long. |
| **Red Phase** | Map-wide color shift at mid-game. | Rely on sound cues more than sight during this phase. |
| **Afton Residence** | A central house structure. | Great for losing hunters in rooms, but easy to get cornered. |
| **Barriers** | Fences and fallen logs. | Use these to vault or loop hunters during a chase. |

### The Warehouse: High-Stakes Close Quarters

Currently, the Warehouse is considered a work-in-progress, but it is already gaining a reputation for being the most difficult of the **bite by night maps**. The layout is designed with tight corridors and cluttered storage rooms, making it nearly impossible to see a hunter until they are right on top of you.

In the Warehouse, escaping a chase is significantly harder than in the Forest or Pizzeria because there are fewer "long" sightlines. Survivors must master "looping"—the act of running around specific obstacles to waste the hunter's time—to survive here. The lack of wide-open spaces means that every corner turned is a potential risk.

## Power Management and Office Mechanics

On maps that include a security office (most notably the Pizzeria), managing your resources is just as important as knowing the floor plan. The office is equipped with a dual-button system on both the left and right sides.

1.  **Top Button (Doors):** Closes the blast door. This is your primary defense but drains power at a high rate.
2.  **Bottom Button (Lights):** Illuminates the hallway immediately outside the door. This allows you to check for "hallway crawlers" without wasting power on the doors.

When exploring **bite by night maps**, you will notice that the power consumption is cumulative. Having both doors closed and both lights on will drain your battery in seconds. The most effective strategy is the "flicker" method: quickly pulse the lights to check for movement, and only drop the doors if you see a hunter mid-stride toward your office.

## General Navigation Tips for All Maps

Regardless of which map you are playing on, certain universal truths apply to the 2026 version of the game. The variety found in **bite by night maps** keeps the gameplay fresh, but it also means you cannot rely on the same trick every round.

*   **Keep Moving:** Staying stationary is the easiest way to get caught. Hunters often check common "hiding" spots first. 
*   **Learn the Loops:** Every map has "looping" locations—areas where you can run in a circle around an object (like a table or a tent) faster than the hunter can turn. 
*   **Use Sound:** Listen for the heavy mechanical footsteps of the animatronics. The audio design in this game is directional, meaning you can often tell exactly which room a hunter is in based on the sound of the floorboards.
*   **Visual Cues:** In the Forest, watch for the swaying of trees or the flickering of lanterns, which can indicate a hunter is nearby even if you can't see them clearly.

## Role-Specific Map Strategies

Different **bite by night maps** require unique strategies depending on whether you are playing as a Survivor or a Hunter. 

### Survivor Strategies
In the Pizzeria, your goal is to bridge the gap between the various rooms. Use the new loading bay to move from the backstage to the exterior of the kitchen without being seen in the main hallway. In the Forest, try to stay near the perimeter until you need to make a move toward a specific objective, as the center of the map is often a "kill zone."

### Hunter Strategies
As a hunter, use the tight spaces of the Warehouse to your advantage. Since survivors have limited escape routes, you can "herd" them into dead ends. On the Forest map, wait for the Red Phase to begin before making your most aggressive moves, as the reduced visibility makes it much easier to sneak up on unsuspecting players.

## FAQ

**Q: Which of the bite by night maps is best for beginners?**

A: The Pizzeria is generally considered the best map for beginners. Its layout is more structured, and the presence of the security office provides a clear "safe zone" where players can learn the basic mechanics of power management and animatronic behavior.

**Q: Is the Warehouse map fully completed?**

A: As of the current 2026 updates, the Warehouse is still considered a work-in-progress. While it is fully playable, developers frequently tweak the layout and add new rooms to balance the difficulty between survivors and hunters.

**Q: How do I survive the Red Phase in the Forest?**

A: The Red Phase is all about sound and memory. Since you can't see as far, you must memorize the location of the Afton residence and the main tents earlier in the match. Use the lights from the lanterns as waypoints to navigate through the darkness.

**Q: Why was Pirates Cove removed from the Pizzeria?**

A: The removal of Pirates Cove was a balance decision. In earlier versions of the **bite by night maps**, Pirates Cove created a bottleneck that was too easy for hunters to exploit. The new entrance room provides a more balanced flow for both teams.
