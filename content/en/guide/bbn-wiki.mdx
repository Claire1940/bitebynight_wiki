## Survivor Classes and Abilities

Survivors in Bite by Night are categorized into distinct classes, each serving a specific role within the team dynamic. Choosing the right class can drastically alter your playstyle and your team's chances of completing objectives before the killer intervenes.

| Class | Difficulty | Primary Role | Key Abilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Customer** | Easy | Survival/Utility | Pizza (Heal), Drink (Stamina) |
| **Medic** | Medium | Team Support | Med Pack (Group Heal), Self-Heal |
| **Fighter** | Hard | Combat/Distraction | Axe Stun, Defensive Parry |
| **Security Guard** | Medium | Intel/Crowd Control | Taser (Slow), Camera Highlights |

### The Customer (Beginner Class)
The Customer is the entry-point for most players. It is a free class that focuses on personal sustain. The Pizza ability provides a health boost that scales based on your current HP—the lower your health, the more effective the heal. However, the Drink ability is a double-edged sword; while it grants a significant stamina duration boost, it leaves the player with a "slowness" debuff once the effect wears off.

### The Medic
A skilled Medic is the backbone of any successful survivor group. Their Med Pack can heal teammates up to 50 HP every two seconds. Interestingly, if a teammate is already at full health, the Med Pack provides "Armor," which effectively increases their maximum health pool up to a cap of 25.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When using the Self-Heal ability as a Medic, your character becomes completely stationary. Always ensure you are hidden or behind a barricade before initiating a heal.

### The Fighter
The Fighter is designed for players who want to go toe-to-toe with the killer. Equipped with an Axe, they can stun the killer to create distance. If you run out of stuns, you must rely on the **Parry** mechanic. A successful parry can negate standard M1 attacks (basic swings), though it will not protect you against specialized projectiles like Springtrap’s axe throw.

## The Killer Roster: Dominating the Hunt

Playing as a killer in BBN requires a deep understanding of map pressure and ability cooldowns. Each killer has a unique kit designed to punish survivor mistakes.

| Killer | Damage (M1) | Playstyle | Unique Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Springtrap** | 36 (Axe) | Aggressive Tracker | Remnant Cleaver & Bear Traps |
| **The Mimic** | 42 (Strength) | Stealth/Ambusher | Mode Switching (Speed/Stealth) |
| **Ennard** | 38 | Tactical/Disguise | Skin Stealer & Wire Eye Minions |

### Springtrap
As the beginner-friendly killer, Springtrap offers a versatile kit. His Remnant Cleaver allows him to throw his axe for ranged damage. Once the axe is thrown, his basic attack damage drops to 28, but he unlocks the ability to place Bear Traps. These traps are essential for ending "loops" where survivors attempt to run in circles around obstacles.

### The Mimic
The Mimic is a high-skill killer that relies on its Mode Switcher. Players can toggle between:
1.  **Strength Mode:** High damage output (42 DMG).
2.  **Speed Mode:** Faster movement but reduced damage (10 DMG).
3.  **Stealth Mode:** Silent movement, unlocking the Grab and Tunneling abilities.

### Ennard
Ennard is perhaps the most complex killer in the 2026 meta. He utilizes "Wire Eyes"—small minions that hunt survivors and highlight their locations. His most dangerous tool is the **Skin Stealer**, which allows him to disguise himself as a fallen survivor or even himself. While disguised, he can perform a Lunge Stab for 45 damage or an AOE Explode attack that stuns everyone in the vicinity.

## Completing Objectives: The Path to Escape

To win a match in Bite by Night, survivors must focus on two primary objective types: Generators and Batteries. Ignoring these will eventually lead to a total team wipe as the killer's Agony increases.

### Generator Repair
Generators are scattered throughout the map. To repair them, you must interact with the unit and complete a mini-game involving connecting plugs and clicking non-red buttons.
*   **Speed Factor:** Multiple survivors working on the same generator significantly increase the repair speed.
*   **Interruption:** If you fail a skill check or are forced to run, the progress remains, but the noise may alert the killer.

### Battery Logistics
Batteries are more mobile objectives. You must pick up a battery and transport it to a specific highlighted area on the map. 
*   **Stamina Management:** Carrying a battery drains stamina.
*   **Quick Drop:** If you hear the killer's heartbeat or a proximity warning, press **X** to drop the battery immediately. This allows you to regain your full movement speed for a chase.

## Advanced Survival Mechanics

Beyond just running and hiding, the **bbn wiki** community has identified several advanced mechanics that separate top-tier players from the rest.

### Barricading and Doors
The barricade mechanic is a vital stalling tool. By holding the **F** key near a door, you can prevent the killer from entering. This initiates a struggle mini-game.
*   **The Struggle:** The killer will punch or swing at the door to break it.
*   **The Stun:** If the killer successfully breaks a door while you are barricading it, you will be stunned for approximately 2 seconds. Always watch the white progress bar; if it drops too low, it is often better to release the door and run before it shatters.

### The Agony System and Store
Agony is the primary currency and scoring metric in Bite by Night. You earn Agony by completing objectives, surviving chases, and helping teammates. 
*   **Lobby Mini-games:** While waiting for a match or after dying, you can play the Snake mini-game in the lobby to pass the time.
*   **The Shop:** Use your accumulated Agony or Robux to purchase new survivors, killers, emotes, and skins. The 2026 update also introduced "Potato Mode" in the settings for users on lower-end hardware, ensuring the game remains accessible to everyone.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your inventory before a match. Equipping specific skins or emotes doesn't just look good—it's a sign of a player who has mastered the Agony grind.

A: The killer cannot see your perspective, but they can see the camera itself moving or glowing. If you highlight the killer, they will receive a warning, so use this ability strategically to help your team without giving away your exact location.
